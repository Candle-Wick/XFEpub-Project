A Breath Choked By Ash
+075.M42+ To +080.M42+
Be Faithful! Be Strong! Be Vigilant! - The Emperor of Mankind​

When the Blood Ravens exited the gravity reef around System Jubacael after weeks of careful travel, there was only a short time spent amongst the rest of their allies. Inquisitor Adrastia had awoken, finally, and was well on her way to recovery. Her appreciation for the live capture of Vandis was considerable, even if she very obviously did not provide any answers as to when or how she had acquired the device used to do so. While the loss of her entire Stormtrooper Regiment stung, and her ship had sustained moderate damage, she frankly assessed that the task had been well worth it. If not for the capture of Vandis, then the destruction of the anchorage itself. On that, at least, all present could agree upon. Admiral Graves, for his part, admitted that he was likely to get quite the haranguing from the Lord Admiral of the Korianis Sector due to the loss of a number of his ships. He was, somewhat, hopeful that the stamp of the Inquisition's approval for his aid would ensure he didn't lose a rank or worse. Adrastia did admit that she found him useful, and for an Inquisitor, that was quite a statement – though for good or ill seemed to depend. The Imperial Guard shuffled off to the only planet named Meridian within several lightyears after a few heartfelt goodbyes. The Blood Ravens, on the other hand, could not afford to do so. Rather, their ships direly required repairs, their Chapter Monastery most of all, and so continued on to the Forge World Zerann. Communications began to pass back and forth by astropath to the rest of the Chapter, and so it was that it was not within Subsector Aurelia, but within Subsector Krasus alongside the Inquisitor and the remaining ships of the Imperial Navy that the Blood Ravens were joined together once more.

Sharing reports was all well and good, but it was another thing entirely to behold each other with their own eyes after but five years or so apart. And so it was, over Zerann, within the nearly shattered wreck that was the Litany of Fury that the Blood Ravens met one another. On the one side was the ragged 5th​ Company, half of the 1st​ Company complement that had been sent after Vandis, and a large number of psykers. On the other, vastly outnumbering them, was the entire rest of the Chapter. As battle brothers joined with one another once more, speaking, telling, joking, commiserating and letting the bonds of brotherhood flourish, the officers turned inwards for a short time. If only to properly take stock with one another and what had occurred in their respective campaigns. The Hunt for Vandis was first of this, told by Gabriel, Justinius, and Tarkus to the rest of the Chapter's upper leadership. There was surprise, sorrow, and pain, but most important of all was the triumph of victory. Even the bitter tang of Mortirex's escape could not taint that overly much.

Then came the tales of the Helba Campaign. Of a Chapter reduced to a single company. Of tyranids in excess of what were faced in the 1st​ Aurelian Crusade. But so too was the Imperial response far greater. The extinction of the Odaken done with, the Order of the Lost Rosetta had turned to their now most hated of foes aside from the forces of Chaos, and had reinforced the Mede Subsector. The Imperial Navy of Helba, as well as the Imperial Guard, had all responded as well. It had been quite a large campaign, for the Mede Subsector was large, the Helba Sector actually closer to the galactic core than the Korianis Sector considerably. As such, a tyranid tendril gaining strength that much closer to Terra demanded extreme responses. World after world was reclaimed, and much information pried loose and disseminated about the new 'Shadow Towers', constructs of flesh and brain meant to expand and empower the Shadow in the Warp to the extent of outright preventing warp travel within any system with said tower. A delaying tactic, the better to allow the Hive Mind time to safely devour. In the end, with Cyrus as Force Commander, the whole of the Order of the Lost Rosetta, the remaining Ebon Claws, and as much force that the Imperial Guard and Navy could muster, the xenos were beat back. Five long, grueling years were spent pruning them from world after world, until reaching their original entry point into Imperial space. There, Cyrus revealed that they had undertaken the direst of gambles, directly going after the Norn-Queens hidden on the enemy bio-ships. It was purely through the aid of their allies, luck, and the experience of Cyrus that victory was achieved and the Chapter survived the fighting at all. Indeed, purely by the numbers, the losses experienced outweighed those of the Blood Ravens fallen fighting against Vandis and his lackeys many times over.

Victory, and victory, purchased in blood. Such was the life of a space marine, set down by the Emperor himself since the founding of the Imperium.

Now there was time, even if only a modicum of it, to rest and recover. The Mechanicus was both shocked and outraged by the presence of a nascent Forge World dedicated to their dark cousins, and for destroying it so utterly it was considered just and fair for them to repair the damage to the ships of the Chapter, including those damaged in the Helba Campaign. Especially the Litany. The very spine of the battle barge's superstructure was a near hair's breadth away from cracking irreparably. Then again, the Fabricator-General declared that sustaining such damage in the course of destroying Opus Maxima meant that such holy wounds demanded immediate and strong attentions. Some things, the loss of a few thunderhawks, could also be replaced in time, either through Zerann or others, but the repair of the ships was far more important. Which was made more significant due to just how incredibly busy the shipyards of Zerann really were. The Imperial Navy desperately needed to replace the ships lost against the Suysh, and there were crash training programs for all levels of crew for such ships apparently being carried out across many of the worlds in Korianis according to the Fabricator-General. As such, the repair of the Litany would take a considerable amount of time, but would be done highly comprehensively, as well as the other ships' repairs.

Assured that those Blood Raven ships that required longer-term repairs would be well guarded by the Mechanicus, the Chapter finally turned towards Subsector Aurelia.

"Governor Derosa," Gabriel returned the smile that the Governor was offering him.

It had taken weeks of work on the part of Martellus, but finally, his bionics were finally fully restored and fine-tuned that much more. He did not garb himself in the mantle of his terminator armor, for that required much longer to fix, but a regular suit of power armor was more than sufficient. He stood at half-attention within her office, the rest of the servants and attendants sent out of the room entirely. The Blood Ravens of the 1st​ Company remained outside as well; a courtesy offered to a close ally of the Chapter. The meeting request had been unexpected, but not unwelcome. Elena Derosa opened her mouth, holding up a glass of some alcohol or another before thinking better of it and placed it on the table on a small coaster of real cork and wood.

"Chapter Master Angelos," she bowed her head to him. "Thank you for meeting with me."

"It is no trouble…," Gabriel trailed off. "Unless it is?"

"Perhaps," she shrugged. "You are aware of the latest cultist problems on Meridian?"

At that, Gabriel's lip curled in distaste.

"I am, yes. More than just usual riots, outright cultists devoted to-,"

"Him, yes," she cut him off, shuddering as even the very thought of the name passed through her. "They are being suppressed well enough by the PDF, but that they haven't stopped – that they're showing up more and more as their very dreams are apparently twisting them around…it is not a good sign. The Ecclesiarchy is sending priests into the Underhive regularly now, trying to buoy their faith, but I do not know if it will be enough."

"A fair consideration," Gabriel rubbed at his chin, walking to join her at one of the enormous windows of her office.

The view of the planet below was lost below the clouds, leaving only a white-grey expanse below and the stars above.

"The very planet of Aurelia was his prison, and it was badly damaged. The Grey Knights, have they done anything more in our absence?"

Derosa waggled a hand.

"They have spent most of their time near Aurelia itself, but have also visited here, on Meridian, as well as Calderis, Typhon, and even…," her irritation softened, "Even Cyrene."

Gabriel sighed.

"They have not offered much reasoning behind their movements, and their authority and position seems enough to scare away anyone loyal to the Emperor from interfering. But-," she was cut off as a rather loud beep came from the console built into her desk. Her expression tightened slightly. "Excuse me," she pressed a rune on the console. "What is it?"

"Governor Derosa, my humblest apologies, but…-,"

"Chapter Master," Gabriel raised an eyebrow as the secure channel vox clicked to life through his comm bead. "Be forewarned, Grey Knights approaching. What are your orders?"

"Calm yourself Sergeant Stromahn," he said quickly even as a now pale-faced Derosa received the same news from her own adjutants. "Do not provoke them."

"…understood, Chapter Master."

"Gabriel," Derosa looked up at him, "I assume you've received the same news as I?"

"Indeed. It seems that Inquisitors and their agents do so love to show themselves without warning," he gave a half-smirk before folding his hands behind his back and turning to face the doors. "Shall we see what they want?"

"It does not appear we have any other choice," she muttered before assuming a similar position. "Let them through, sergeant," she muttered to her own household guard.

Less than a minute passed before the Grey Knights arrived, pushing through the doors. There were only three of them, but each radiated considerable psychic might. Their leader bore a force halberd, the fierceness of his gaze palpable as he glanced from Governor to Chapter Master and back again.

"Justicar Tolmen," Derosa bowed curtly.

"Governor Derosa," the lead Grey Knight grunted. "We bear grave tidings, best delivered by person rather than by other…more easily intercepted methods. Chapter Master, this involves you as well," he said with a slight aside glance. "The Ordo Malleus had finished its investigations and studies. Based upon all of the data gathered, there is only one conclusion – the daemon is beginning to force open the bars of his cage."

Gabriel grit his teeth as Derosa leaned down heavily upon her desk.

"It is as we suspected then," she sighed. "Do we…is there any possible…?"

"The sealing ritual relied upon the planet being whole. It is not. Its freedom is, unfortunately, inevitable."

"So there is nothing to be done, then?" Gabriel asked as Derosa cursed quietly to herself.

The Justicar slowly turned his head to face the Chapter Master.

"There is already chatter that his influence is calling those devoted to the Fly Lord towards him, reports of certain devotees leaving their bases behind. We are already doing what we can to reinforce the seals – elsewise he would have already broken free," the Grey Knight said flatly. "Some few years, half a decade, perhaps. Then we will have to contest him outright."

"Along with all the flotsam that he can draw to his side by that point," Gabriel said, eyebrows furrowed.

"Correct."

"Do we have any idea as to who might listen to his call?" Derosa asked without looking upwards from her desk. "Or who already has?"

"Some," Tolmen tilted his head towards one of his brother marines, the other Grey Knight stepping forward to speak in a curiously raspy voice.

"Two warbands have recently abandoned their holdings ahead of multiple strike teams sent to hit them – the Plaguebones, formerly known as the Snakebones, and the Death Bringers. Both former loyal Chapters to the Imperium that fell to Chaos."

Gabriel let the information wash over him, the dismay a heavy weight which settled on his soldiers.

"Neither was at full strength at the time of their excommunications," the second Grey Knight continued, "But together, they are a considerable foe. There may be more, it is difficult to tell."

"If we know they are coming, we can prepare our forces to stop them then!" Derosa said, eyes bright with hope which withered as the looked at the impassive Grey Knights. "Can we not?"

"Given current circumstances throughout much of the Imperium, re-dedicating significant quantities of the Imperium's forces for a long period of time from their other duties may prove disastrous."

Derosa's head drew back in shock.

"So we're to be abandoned?"

"No. We cannot allow a daemon of this strength free reign, even this far on the Eastern Fringe," Tolmen shook his head. "Our Brotherhood shall arrive when the warnings we have inscribed on the seals activate, and the Blood Ravens will presumably be on hand to aid in the coming fight. If you have any other allies to call upon," he glanced at Gabriel, "It may be time to do so, if only that they may prepare themselves."

Their warning delivered, the Grey Knights swiftly departed, leaving Derosa and Gabriel behind to contemplate the future. Elena elected to do so with the fortifying drinking of three flutes of some bubbly alcohol, though Gabriel instead simple cast his mind out and reviewed what forces the Blood Ravens possessed.

"Half of the Meridian regiments are deployed elsewhere and cannot possibly be called back. A fourth are still in training, and the other fourth recuperating after Vandis," she spoke up eventually. "And I do not trust the Penal Legions to fight properly."

"You have Penal Legions?" Gabriel raised an eyebrow.

"I do now," she nodded. "Food and water riots, the greatest banes to Meridian's ongoing functioning under Vandis, have dropped more than 80%."

"Most precipitous, Governor."

"Yes, well," she did not smile, merely shrugged. "It helps when the hungry and thirsty are fed and watered…so long as they depart the planet forever."

Gabriel considered that for a moment.

"None of the Penal Legions of Meridian are slated to return, are they," he stated the truth he'd realized.

"No. They are recombined, reorganized, and sent out continually. Productivity has never been higher, crime and riots are a small fraction of what they once were, all at the comparatively low cost of equipping and training them before deploying them wherever Segmentum Command wishes. And yet, I shudder to think what the daemon might do if they were brought into the fight here."

"I understand. I must return to my battle brothers. Be well, Governor."

"You as well, Angelos."

"We all knew it was coming," Justinius mused as the rest of the Chapter's leadership met once more. "At least we have a vague timeline."

"Should we try and fortify Calderis and Meridian?"

"We can try," Gabriel frowned, "But their main target will be to destroy Aurelia, and in doing so, free Ulkair."

"The good Governor is no doubt heavily fortifying Meridian, and unfortunately there is little we can do for Calderis," Cyrus grunted, "It's too wide and open, any major fortifications would become an obvious target, even if it was only to try and evacuate the civilians."

"Argus cannot hold all of the people of Calderis regardless," Tarkus shook his head. "Our best bet would be to position troops there if we are to defend it at all, and with what settlements we can…and still the many nomad clans will be difficult to herd in."

"A fair point," Gabriel allowed. "As for allies, perhaps we may call upon the Order of the Lost Rosetta. We have our differences, but their homes are a short distance away from here, they cannot ignore the threat that the daemon and its slaves may pose."

"What of the Scarlet Lions?" Captain Marcus brought up, his hellishly grotesque face twisting in thought. "They were capable and stalwart."

"And thoroughly infiltrated by the Alpha Legion," Cyrus pointed out before sighing, "Still, they may come to our aid…if they are not otherwise engaged."

"Are there any others?" Gabriel asked, looking around the room before sighing. "If any of you have further thoughts, come and inform me."
