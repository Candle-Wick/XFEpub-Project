The ork tumbled down the hill, Margaret on top of him, as the sky was lit by inferno and bolter barrages falling upon the orks. Soon enough, the greenskins broke, their bastion burnt to a crisp, and their leaders slain from afar. But they could not run quick enough, or far enough, for waiting on the other end of the tree line that the orks retreated to, waited a dozen more penitent engines, their screaming heretical pilots driven to seek holy repentance through death.

Boiling hatred for the alien filling her, the Canoness slowly stomped back up the hill, hand going to her ear as the vox implant crackled.

“It is done. The valley is cleared. We are moving out across the rest of the planet now, separating into teams.”

“Thank you for your hard work, Canoness.”

“I have no accomplishments to my name, General, only service to the Emperor. How goes your work upon the ships reclaimed for the Emperor’s service?”

“Well enough Canoness. More troops will be landing upon Spinesnap shortly.”
