Echoes of Genocide
+065.M42+ To +070.M42+
+Through The Foe's Obliteration Do We Earn Salvation+​

The warp crackled and tore as the Suysh ships emerged, their rocky and asymmetrical exteriors trailing eldritch energies that quickly dissipated as the laws of physics reasserted themselves. The Sacred Crown’s advanced auspex sensors, restored to the Imperium’s nominal control, painted them the moment they did so. There was a screech of fury from the queen residing within the hulking Scorchnest, its attendant cruisers and escort ships riled up by their progenitor’s anger at the sight of the still floating remains of the Suysh fleet which had once patrolled the solar system. Hadrian spared a handful of seconds towards a bridge servitor’s warning lights of an incoming transmission. The tracked source was, predictably, the enemy battleship.

“BLOOD-ROT UPON YOU, THIEVES, MURDERERS, TAINTED MEAT! SISTER VOID-SLAKE’S DEATH-OATH SHALL BE FULFILLED! YOU WILL BE OUR MEAT! SO SWEARS LEXTAL STARFEASTER! MEAT! MEAT! MEA-,”

A twitch of attention from his place in the command throne and the vox channel closed. Hadrian simply raised an eyebrow as he glanced both at the tactical screen at the foot of the command throne and arcane systems of the throne itself. Through the venerable sensors and cameras of the Omnis, he saw the xeno fleet burning its engines to get into combat range. Around his ship, however, came the rest of the Blood Ravens fleet. They readied themselves, engine-hearts burning fiercely, and he felt a tight grin come across his face as the bombardment cannons began to ready themselves as well. If the Suysh wished to frame and build their vessels as if they were earthen, as if they were planetoid? They would be cracked apart like them too.

“Attention all ships,” Hadrian’s fingers tightened over the throne’s edges as he leaned forward. “Fire at will!”

Once more, the void between the planets and star of the system were lit up by weapons fire.

“Come and die, xenos. This is the Emperor’s space. You are not welcome,” he hissed beneath his breath.

Not every ship under Blood Raven control proceeded to the fight brewing on the edge of the system. The 2nd​ Company fleet, newly assembled as it was, remained over the worlds and continued to batter them down with unceasing bombardment fire. This was what they were, quite literally, made for. Two strike cruisers they might have been, but for many a world throughout the warring galaxy one was enough. But perhaps just as important were their teleportariums, systems which sizzled and popped with unnatural airs and electricity as the techmarines and lesser Blood Raven psykers maintained their continued function. Of the four worlds to be reclaimed, it was Matsuya where a Queen had been properly located. Found thanks to the works of Captain Sicarius, and so it was he who earned the honor of leading the charge while the rest of the Chapter continued to fight on the other planets to keep them from organizing properly – though elements of every company were sent, of course, for given the records of the Deathwatch, they would need the help.

Bluevein Mountain was the place, its hollowed out innards large enough to provide a safe home for the Queen that now inhabited it. Its mines were now resources for the alien, rather than humanity, and this could be allowed no longer. It had, in its time under the reptilian grip of the Suysh, become quite the bastion for the foe. Guard towers, sensor arrays, artillery, even patrolling aircraft were present. Here, there would be a number of the elites that could not have been found in the columns and bases elsewhere. But Captain Sicarius did not care. How could he? He had a target, and he had his brothers beside him. A number of the 10th​ Company had even left their own assigned planet, momentarily at least, granting him aid in the form of Captain Cyrus who willingly subordinated himself for a time.

Bluevein Mountain Skirmishes
Infantry - 12 vs. 1
Vehicles - 32 vs. 12

First came the distractions. Assaults along the edges, utilized thoroughly by the vehicles that would be far less effective in the tighter quarters of the mines. There were rapidly redeploying assault marines on jump packs appearing from the skies before departing back behind entrenched tactical marine lines. Wedges of predator tanks and razorbacks drove up, fired, and reversed away as the Suysh attempted to retaliate properly. From every direction around the mountain came the probes, the scorched and burnt remains of former defensive structures long-destroyed by the efforts of the 2nd​ Company aiding such things. Assault bikes came screaming over the wasteland, while above the thunderhawks rained fiery death when they were not fighting it out with Suysh aircraft.

Such a thing could not continue forever, of course.

Bluevein Mountain Insertions
86 vs. 16

But when the moment was right, the Librarius sensing the moment that the defenses parted in a handful of specific locations, the 2nd​ Company struck. The targets were a trio of entrances into the mines, their defenders momentarily reassigned, all before more reinforcements could arrive or bubble up from the depths. The whole of the 2nd​ Company tore through the Suysh with blistering long range weapons fire keeping them pinned in place for assault marines to leap down and slaughter those who hunkered down behind rock and steel. Captain Sicarius personally led the charge, firing his plasma cannon as he went, each blast of superheated death lighting the way as they fought their way into the tunnels. At the same time, the 1st​ Company, led by Gabriel, fought ever fiercer on the outside, their terminator-clad bodies presenting just as much if not more of a threat, forcing the Suysh to split their attentions even further. Such was the death they granted to so many xenos that a higher number of hungerers and royal guard emerged from the tunnels to strike under open air than remained within the mountain.

Flamers of both xeno and Imperial design soon illuminated the caves, transforming a darkness that the Suysh could navigate without issue into a scorching light-filled hellscape. The Suysh knew why the enemy had come. Of course they did. This was the home of one of their Queens, and so the foe had come to kill her. They, rather predictably, began to swarm in defense of their precious hive. The most elite troops the alien possessed were sent forth, hulking monstrosities of hardened scales and inhuman musculature wielding weapons that even some marines would struggle to hold as easily. Dreadnought fought dreadnought-sized aliens, stomping tombs beginning to glow yellow under the sheer heat, granting the half-dead marine within horrifying sensation as their shriveled husks began to almost boil within the fluids that surrounded them. Chaplain Ravias, absent from the Fist of Faith that fought elsewhere in the system, bellowed vile curses and incandescent hatred towards the alien as he rallied those who stumbled from their wounds.

Insertion had relied on a multi-pronged assault, rather than simply hoping that a single push could get the Blood Ravens inside. But after that had been achieved, they needed to try and regroup within. This proved to be harder than expected for one group, a number of 2nd​ Company marines taking wounds as the Suysh tore and bit at their ceramite. In one case, a marine had his armor stripped entirely from one of his arms, the meat within then subsequently turned to melted flesh and bone, a waxy mockery of a true appendage that required immediate amputation. Elsewhere, Captain Sicarius and Captain Octavius alternatively shot a hole through the enemy or somehow managed to simply slip past. The cavern they found themselves in was one large enough to bear the scars of the Queens passing, a massive chamber which had once simultaneously been an ore processing plant and barracks for slave labor who would have never seen the light of day in their entire lives.

“Captain,” Sicarius nodded briefly before twisting about and firing his weapon once more, unleashing a sphere of blinding light that soon erupted as it struck a royal guard in the chest.

“Captain,” Cyrus grunted back as he fired again and again with his rifle, pausing for a moment to trigger a series of planted detonation packs which reduced a Suysh charge into nothing more than bloody chunks.

Truly, it was unsettling how the Suysh were happy enough to gobble the giblets that remained of their brethren. A vast number of bloodscales had maws that were drenched in the ichor of their own kind, simply racing forward on all four limbs to get to the fight. A method of travel that easily allowed them to duck their head momentarily and scarf up as much meat as they could along the way. Others, those wounded, were similarly cannibalized by their fellows. The moment a Suysh was too crippled to fight, but not yet dead, those around them would reach over and tear out gobs of meat and bone for devouring, their weapons and gear seamlessly redistributed amongst the rest of their ‘squads’. More than one bloodscale now sported extra rifles and incendiary grenades than they had begun with.

“Are we ready to descend?”

Cyrus simply nodded. The beacons were not too large, not like the bulky constructs that Boreale had demanded for his fighting, a chain rather than an island unto themselves. Sicarius fired again, bellowing for ammo to be brought up to him and then leapt down from the higher pile of rocks and ruined ore machinery he’d found himself atop. He paused only to slap the blood-covered pauldron of his chaplain before advancing. But that was signal enough. Ravias lifted his crozius high, its power fields still sparking and lashing out after being slammed into a charging hungerer’s chest and out through the back. The advanced systems within the chaplain’s armor came to life, his vox-cast booming out on every Blood Raven channel.

“Brothers! Into the darkness we go! Into the foul alien hive we descend! We bring the Emperor’s light! We bring his fury! For we are, all of us,” he paused to smash the legs from beneath another hungerer and twisted his weapon about to obliterate its skull without stopping his movement, “Blood Ravens! We are the Angels of Death! MAY NONE FIND US WANTING!”

A resounding cheer went up, and the Blood Ravens responded.

“MAY NONE FIND US WANTING!”

Descent 1
30 vs. 81

As a concentrated mass, the Blood Ravens struck deep, descending through passages caked with alien filth and architecture. It would be decades of work on the part of the Imperium to truly excise every remnant of their presence from this place. Hovels where noble human miners had once worked for twenty hours a day were now home to whole swarms of Suysh who curled about each other to share body heat when they were not on deployment. Machines that had once been blessed weekly by the Mechanicus now groaned under the weight of their alien modifications, forced into battle by raging Suysh who did all they could to force the invaders back. Yet, such was the capabilities of high quality ceramite that rather than melting innards, than boiling away armor, the Blood Ravens were simply left glowing under the immense heat more than anything else. For the Suysh, it was as if dozens of beings made of pure light were fighting against them.

To be sure, however, many a weapon of the Blood Ravens were rendered inoperable either from damage or from the danger of continued use. Even the highest quality of plasma weaponry was delicate in terms of heat distribution at the best of times, and so even the Captain of the 2nd​ had to resort to putting away his relic weaponry and took up the venerable bolter once more to continue the fight. Where ceramite provided, flesh happened to fail. The Suysh began to charge in greater and greater number, overwhelming squads and tearing ceramite apart under their own mediocre power weaponry. More than one marine felt his skin begin to blacken and flake away where the ceramite could no longer provide protection. Barrels began to deform, thermal sensors becoming less than useless at the same time.

Descent 2
43 vs. 50

Minutes stretched into hours. Hours stretched into days. Momentum for both sides began to slow as they ground against one another. The mountain became a charnel pit within the depths. It did not matter how many times an alien died, for the Suyush devoured all – bone, skin, meat, it made no differences. The ground could not become carpeted with bodies, for there were almost no bodies present. The Blood Ravens found themselves forced into ever tighter quarters, collapsing tunnels that would require much work to clear whenever the Imperium came upon them to restore the mines properly. Tertiary concerns, for ones such as space marines, but each was an outlying failure, at least in the mind of Captain Sicarius. Rather than a great plunging spear of death, they had been blunted again and again. Each time the Blood Ravens tried to redirect, to follow a new portion of the mine network, they found their efforts stymied. Blood was shed, apothecaries desperately worked to keep wounded marines alive. More than one who had found themselves wounded into suspended animation was revived not back in a proper apothecarium but upon the battlefield itself, their wounds patched just enough to return them to being able to at least fire a bolter or carry supplies.

“We must push through!” A sergeant cried before he was buried in bodies.

It took precious moments for the squad around him to slay every one of the creatures piling atop him, retrieving a bloodied but still living sergeant from beneath. Roaring, he revved his gore-clogged chainsword as he rose and threw it into the throat of a charging Suysh. Thankfully its momentum carried the soon-to-be corpse right back to him so he could grasp it around the hilt and tear it away. A vigorous shaking for a few seconds to clear the teeth was all he had time for before another mass of bodies hit him, heat-blasters and thermal cannons causing his exposed wounds to bubble and smoke. It seemed to do little but annoy the veteran of the 1st​ Company rather severely.

“This isn’t working!” A newly promoted marine cried, “Push them ba - argh!”

A stalker had gotten close, its stealth capabilities letting it dance around the press of bodies and cut out his throat. Though it scabbed over quickly, the marine nonetheless was momentarily dropped to his knees as his body worked overtime to keep the wound from being a fatal one.

Gabriel ground his teeth as he remembered the revelation that Cyrus had brought him.

“Well, we were going to have words about her choice in tools – there will be recompense for what her ‘aid’ have brought us,” he growled out. “For they have arrived and already left, having done little to aid us at all! This only means our ‘discussion’ will be sooner.”

“Chapter Master, that’s just it,” Martellus sounded surprised as he continued to read the message in print form, “I fear we have been deceived.”

That was enough to give Gabriel pause while the rest of the Honor Guard fought around him.

The emblem of the Scarlet Lions was emblazoned across the vessel, its designation finding its way into the cogitators seconds later. From his place in the Sacred Crown with its advanced sensory technologies linked with those of the orbital stations, the Master of the Forge winced as he saw the stress fractures and flickering to the void shielding which would no doubt require long hours of repair. Void Resolution
32 vs. 25

The last of the Suysh reinforcement ships hung in the void, shattered to the last. Such was their frustratingly sturdy construction that the Mechanicus would likely be able to obtain many a sample from the debris field that had been created. Hadrian grit his teeth at the damage reports for some of the ships, but overall they were all perfectly stable and ready for battle once more if required. He would, of course, prefer that every vessel under his command came out pristine from every conflict, but that was a passing fancy, an impossibility that he spared but a handful of seconds to dream of. As it was, with Martellus doing some patchwork at the Sacred Crown, the techmarines and servitors already present on the ships, and a few weeks, they could leave the system any time. But with interdiction work complete, he could at least return to bombardment efforts.

“Captain,” a servitor buzzed from somewhere on the bridge. “We are receiving a communication from one of the Scarlet Lion vessels – identified as the Purity’s Blade.”

“Put them through,” he muttered, attention splitting itself to take care of a dozen tasks besides the conversation.

“Salutations, fellow sons of the Emperor!”

Hadrian blinked, his mind momentarily derailed by both the words and archaic gothic.

“Greetings…?” Hadrian let his question continue into the silence.

“Ah! A folly of mine own enthusiasm,” the speaker said with tight and disturbingly genuine recrimination in their voice. “I doth have the honor to be Captain Kallek, of the 2nd​ Company of the Scarlet Lions!”

Hadrian’s attention slowly began to turn more and more weight to the conversation. For he knew, as did all of the captaincy and others amongst the leadership, that such a thing was required to be patently false lest another to have deceived them. Almost unbidden, twitches of his thoughts and wishes were translated by the command throne into silent and secure communications with the rest of his ships. Steadily, the Blood Raven fleet extracted itself from the debris field and formed up, making sure to keep the Scarlet Lion vessels in front of them at all times.

“Captain? Not master?” He said instead of the immediate accusations that came to mind.

“Ah? Hah, no,” Kallek said with a slowly fading boisterousness, “In the manner of the Lion’s sons, eh? No. Well, perhaps,” he replied slowly, contemplation obvious through the static-filled vox. “It is still up for debate.”

“What do you mean?”

Hadrian steadily began to blink out a call to alert and arms amongst his company once more, standing down his original schedule of having them return to quarters. This might yet, he knew, come to a new fight.

“Ah, it is but to our shame that the Scarlet Lions know not our Primarch,” Kallek sighed loudly with a painful grief. “Tis an old wound, verily, suffered sometime in the 34th Millenium that our homeworld was destroyed by the vagaries of Chaos, demanding that we be bound ‘pon the stars, whilst simultaneously destroying much of our records and histories of the past.”

Hadrian just stared at the tactical screen displaying the Scarlet Lions fleet that slowly floated past in the void.

“And none others remembered?”

It was not the question he wanted to ask, but it was the one that came out first. Now all of him was intensely focused upon the speaker. He felt the old ache, the painful urge, the blazing fire that had consumed so many Blood Ravens in the past. Over the possibility, the question, the absolute certainty that the information was out there, and that they simply had to find it. One way or another, the Blood Ravens would know their origin, and here, then, was yet another chapter who knew not had been their progenitor?

“In part, in parcel,” Kallek admitted, “References to our name, our heroes and champions, in the records to other chapters, but none as to that question’s answer so specifically.”

It was not another denial, another Inquisitor denying information, and yet Hadrian felt the wound to his heart as if it had been.

“We, too, know not our Primarch,” he said through suddenly dry lips. “We…have searched for so long…”

“Ah. Then I can do naught but wish thine efforts luck, rather more than us Scarlet Lions. By the order of Chapter Master Platon in the 35th​ Millennium, we ended ours.”

That, more than anything, brought Hadrian up short. His mind simply could not grasp, in those first few seconds, the words that had just been spoken.

“Excuse me?”

“He reason’d that it didst not matter,” Kallek said, his previous cheer slowly returning to him, “We art still yet loyal sons of the Emperor. We still serve His Imperium. We couldst and shall not doth nothing more than honor Him in that.”

There were a number of blinking lights in the back of his mind, insistent requests for confirmation on whether or not to attack or prepare defenses, whether to reassign techmarines and servitors from hull repair to weapon readying. He both saw them and saw them not.

“But you do not know!” He almost hissed, his voice becoming something quite unlike his normal voice.

“Aye, thy words be truth,” Kallek somehow managed to communicate a shrug with those words. “But the writings of Platon are required writings for all initiates to memorize.”

Then he coughed slightly, clearing his throat.

“‘And know this, mine brothers, there doth not exist a shame greater than to fail thine Emperor, to besmirch His legacy. We must, therefore and henceforth, never do so. Our father be without name, and yet we know the Emperor, and thus, who we wilt honor. Thine blood, thine gene-seed, be His legacy, before any others. Know this for sooth, and know that no other detail of origin matters. We are the Emperor’s chosen, his Angels of Death. We are his Scarlet Lions,’” Kallek finished his reciting with reverence.

“But-,”

“But aye, enough of such things!”

The engines on the Scarlet Lion ships flared as they began heading towards the planets.

“We hath come to assist in thine retrieval of these worlds in His name, not merely pontificate! Let us engage in glorious battle together!”

Hadrian twitched before ordering his ships to turn about and do the same as their peers.

“And you know you are not of the Lion’s sons?”

“A most intriguing subject, forsooth, one mind brethren have debated for generations, but alas, the sons of the Lion say they know us not,” Kallek managed to verbally shrug once more. “In truth, our name cometh from the now extinct Lemnian scarlet lions, whose mighty frames once stalked the red grasses of our homeworld Lemnos. Before Chaos came, that is.”

For a week and a half, the Blood Ravens had been stymied in their efforts within the Bluevein Mines. No longer. With the Scarlet Lions supporting them, they plunged into the deeps with absolute purpose and unshakeable fury. For all that the Suysh defenses grew ever more desperate, more and more bodies being thrown in the way, they could not be stopped. Three space marine captains, two of Blood Raven origin and the third from the strange Scarlet Lions, led their combined forces deep within, one mile, then another beneath the surface of the world. Every variety of the alien was represented in those deeps, all serving the will of their Queen. Clawborn in their tens of thousands, whose vast numbers could not be put to proper work due to the tightness of the tunnels the space marines traveled through, the caverns that the blew apart. Bloodscales, veterans, who found all their experience shredding apart Imperial Guard doing nothing to prepare them for the fury of dozens of power sword-wielding Scarlet Lions. Crimson crashed into scarlet, and one hue soon overwhelmed the other.

One level after another they went down, any mine elevators an absolute impossibility to make use of due to the surety of sabotage and open vulnerability to enemy attacks. Instead, they trudged their way through filth-ridden tunnels, transforming alien barracks into slaughterhouses, abattoirs in revenge for the many humans killed here by xeno hands. They took the paths that had been carved into the rock and dirt first, before the elevators could be built, through older mine shafts that had been closed off due to having been exhausted. Here, they found hungerers, the ogryn-sized brutes roaring out of the shadows. Heat-blade struck power-sword, primitive and divergent technology managing to hold up to the ancient blades that the Scarlet Lions wielded. In the past month, not only Cyrus but every marine of the 2nd​ Company had heard a great many tales of the Scarlet Lions. An apparent philosopher in their Chapter Master Platon, who had died fighting in one of the Black Crusades. Of the ‘Naiser Crusade’, one that Cyrus had never heard of but had dealt with over a hundred worlds brought back into the Imperium’s light. The hunting down and slaying of the Chaos warband who had been responsible for destroying the world of Lemnos, the homeworld of the Scarlet Lions, thousands and thousands of years ago.

Winds Of Death
71

The winds of the cavern blew one way, then the other. The continual explosions, the general heat levels that would grant the touch of heat stroke to mortal men without specialized equipment, and further holes bored into the earth were the creators of this. But there was a pattern to them, one that had been painstakingly observed during the travel. One that his mind had calculated in a long-running process since the moment he had begun ascent. One that, even now, he quadruple-checked before nodding. Tensing, he curled his body inwards and then out, beginning to swing himself back and forth. In. Out. In. Out. Then, at the exact right moment, he let go. Death fell with nothing more than a quiet beep to announce itself. A blast of wind, the result of a hundred tanks firing, of thermal drafts blooming from cannons booming, and it whipped directly opposite the direction he’d thrown it. This, of course, was planned. For after continuing to fall, it twisted about in the air as it was tugged in another direction entirely by the drafts coming from the northern tunnels of the mine, who had consistently released such with absolute precision within a miniscule second difference from each instance to the next. There was no such divergence here either, at least none worth noting.

Cyrus watched and waited, barely bothering to breathe, his face an absolute study in concentration.

Eventually, the package achieved terminal velocity.

Down it went, its descent only somewhat arrested here and there as it was shifted horizontally by the winds, but never was its progress truly stymied. One minute passed. Then another. The distance was considerable, after all. But he knew explosives better than anyone in the rest of the entire chapter, save perhaps Martellus who by dint of his position knew secrets of Mars that not even Cyrus was personally privy to. There was nothing more than a quiet beep to mark its passage through the darkness of the cavern, even when it passed from darkness into fell light borne of xeno-inspired industry and life. The attendant army that encircled the Queen were enough noise pollution on their own, thousands of feet stomping, tanks rumbling, hiss-clicks of their foul speech filling the air. None of which was as loud as the disgusting wheezes of the Queen herself, or the regular bursts of mucous and slime from her laying orifices which were continually producing new egg clusters. Around her were entire clusters of alien psykers, the warp screamers bathed in psychic fires or wreathed in discolored lightning.

It was an army capable of matching one of the Imperium’s, in many a circumstance. A full Imperial Crusade, perhaps, could crush and destroy their race. But stretched as the Imperium was in all directions, one simply could not be marshaled for such an effort. Not when Armageddon teetered on the brink of destruction, when all of the realm of Ultramar was embroiled in conflict with Hive Fleet Leviathan, when so many battlefields and crusades had already been called across the galaxy. But such a thing was, perhaps, not needed.

Or at least, that was what Cyrus thought when the combined explosives of the entire 2nd​ Company of the Blood Ravens and many of the 4th​ Company of the Scarlet Lions, along with scavenged Suysh munitions impacted the top of the Suysh Queen’s head and exploded. Plasma and melta and vortex combined into a concurrent explosion of epic proportions, and in a resounding series of shockwaves which made many of the closest aliens around the Queen being thrown to their feet. Despite the grim captain’s words, it was of course not nearly the same level of detonation achieved by the immensely powerful plasma bomb that had once been used on the planet of Kronus. For all that it nearly shook all of the scouts off of the roof that they clung to with desperate strength.

But it was close enough.

Once the light had faded, the Queen from the waist up was gone, leaving only charred and smoking ruin behind.

“Return, return now!” Cyrus shouted suddenly, and the teleportarium beacons began to flicker and vibrate heavily as the warp was once more torn open.

The techmarines clustered around the largest assembled repeater shouted the same as no one in the entire cavern could possibly have missed the explosion. High in orbit, the ships responded to the signal that appeared on their consoles. Within seconds, they began to activate. For a wonder, the Suysh had ceased their attacks entirely, as it turned out, allowing a great many of the wounded to be pulled to their feet so that a teleportarium could pull groups out. From the largest of the royal guard, to the clawborn who were just beginning to push their way out of their wet shells, every xeno visible had paused in their movement. This hesitance lasted but a moment, but for Trajan Maphar, it was an eternity. It was worse, as it turned out, due to his lack of injury putting him towards the end of the priority list on evacuation protocol.

The 10th​ Company epistolary could only scream as his eyes and began to gush blood, his entire body locking up from the titanic forces being unleashed just on the other side of the veil of reality.

For he saw, in truth, the form of a Suysh Queen.

What had been a bloated pathetic thing in flesh was a horror of magnificence in the warp. The leviathan-sized soul of the Suysh Queen, already being torn asunder by predators and harvester creatures who desired any and all possible sustenance. Its body twisted and writhed under the attentions of those that would soon end it, but he saw, truly, what it was. Laced psycho-reactive pheromones spread throughout its existence, coating and lasting far beyond pure organic possibilities. Spread by every royal guard, themselves possessors of symbiotic growths grown alongside and within them by the very nature of their targeted growth. He watched, and heard, as the Suysh Queen’s presence thinned and focused itself, bending the tides of the immaterium with a strength that not even the Chief Librarian could possibly have possessed for a single instant.

There were no words, only intent.

Vengeance and hatred, all targeted, impossibly, by one of the first teleported out of the cavern.

Despite never seeing Cyrus Octavius, never knowing he was there, for that brief instant after physical death before the soul was disassembled, the Queen knew him.

And then so did her hive.

The sheer effort of such a thing burnt the massive soul of the Queen away entirely, leaving not even embers left behind while simultaneously scorching the many daemons who had come to try and feast. With its completion, the Suysh left within the mines screamed so loudly as to burst a space marine’s ear drums within his helmet. Their very flesh split open as something inside of their bodies activated, musculature engorging, fangs and claws lengthening, their very bodies being altered by the death scream, their Queen’s final command. Then they turned as one and began to charge, weapons aside from their own blades left behind. Tanks gunned their engines and started running over those who could not get out of the way. VTOLs accelerated at top speed to initiate suicide crashes.

Only then, as one of the last, was Trajan returned from within Bluevein Mountain to arrive gasping and choking on his own blood on the floor of the Howling Hatred’s teleportarium.

Force Commander Bartorius slammed his fist onto the side of the table, but said nothing as he listened to the audio logs provided by the Blood Ravens. All that was required now was for it to be directed.
