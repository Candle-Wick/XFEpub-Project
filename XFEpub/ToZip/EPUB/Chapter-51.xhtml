To Rise From Ash And Blood
+010.M42+ To +015.M42+
+Our War Is Eternal+​

“And that,” Theodosia booms, “IS THAT!”

Atop the Last Scream Of Aerus, as the now shattered Imperator-sized mass of warp touched scrap metal and machinery had been named, the vaguely dreadnought shaped Magos Prime cuts an admittedly imposing figure. Or perhaps it is the multiple tons of hyper advanced weaponry that bedecks her frame that is what could be more intimidating to the foes of mankind. To be sure, the fact that multiple missile launchers, assault cannons, plasma calivers, heavy flamers, and a greater number of weapons that you know not the names for is also likely to do the same. She whirls, somewhat slowly, to face you with the great bulk of her metallic figure, pneumatics hissing under the weight of her weaponry and armor, and though you cannot actually see any part of her that resembles a face anymore amidst all the weaponry she sounds cheerful.

“CHAPTER MASTER!” she greets, “A GLORIOUS BATTLE IN THE NAME OF THE OMNISSIAH!!!”

“Indeed,” you answer with a comparatively much quieter response. “With this, the last of Aerus has finally been pacified.”

Five years. Five years of grueling battle. You had fought alongside the Mechanicum throughout them all, space marines shedding blood alongside the servants of the Omnissiah, and witnessing such things as you have never before. A greater abundance of advanced weaponry you have rarely seen, only to be matched by the necron and eldar. Conversion weaponry, grav guns and plasma fusils and plasma blasters, and far more. You witnessed the Baneblade of Zerann brought onto the field to join it’s Fellblade cousin, both of which dwarfed the two land raiders that fought alongside them all the same. If anything, it was almost humbling to be but aid in a war against a maddened world while the Mechanicus led the way.

And yet, even then, not all had been successful. Blood Ravens had fallen, not just injured but brought to a point beyond the ability of a dreadnought to preserve. Dozens and dozens of tanks have been irreparably lost, melted to slag. The 1st​ Meridian Consolidated, at the end of five years, has been reduced to a mere ¼ of its original strength. Those that remain are hard-bitten veterans of a fight wherein combat was daily, who have scoured themselves of traitors and mutating brethren time and again with the aid of your own brothers, that of your Chaplains and their priests. But from that core of veterans shall come, in time, a greater whole than there was before. It is something to perhaps take some heart in. Unlike Isador, without hope for mankind, you know that it would have fallen long ago.

“AS TO YOUR REQUEST,” Theodosia stomps from side to side, the sheer size of her form making even simple pacing a rather thunderous affair, “…I ACCEPT YOUR REASONING! IT IS LOGICAL!”

Then a great scattering of binary booms out from her various speakers, and a servitor that would be wholly clerical based on its onboard equipment save for the multiple gun-limbs sticking out of it to accompany its pen and parchment trundles forth on treads. With a vague amount of ceremony, it jerkily bows to both you and Theodosia before scrawling out a rather lengthy amount of text upon its sheaf in what appears to be alternatively be black oil, blood, or both. After a few moments, the rest of your Chapter assembling in the background to observe as you stand atop the corpse of the titan alongside the leader of the Mechanicus on this world, it finishes.

“FOR YOUR AID, BOTH IN FORCES CONTRIBUTED,” and here she lowers her voice to a mere shout, “As well as consistent near-personal sacrifice of your greatest of champion and yourself,” and then she scales back up to bone-rattling volume, “SO SWEAR I, ARCHIMANDRITE OF ZERANN 52X1 AND ARCHMAGOS PRIME AND NOW DECLARANT RULER FABRICATOR GENERAL OF AERUS, THAT A PACT IS FORGED IN OIL AND BLOOD AND BROTHERHOOD BETWEEN FELLOW SEEKERS OF KNOWLEDGE AND DESTROYERS OF TECHNO-HERESY!”

Then, to your utter bemusement, the tiniest and most delicate of handling appendages comes from a slot just beneath one of her still smoking assault cannons to take up the quill and sign upon the last portion of the newly written treaty before offering it to you. In turn, you do the same, and once complete the entire sheaf is rolled up and presented to you with another jerky bow of the servitor that then retreats. With that completed and done with, the now apparently Fabricator-General of Aerus turns back to you, some of her weapons now slowly receding into the depths of her behemoth body.

“You shall taste of my forges until time such as our pact is broken, or our world destroyed,” she says with such suddenly calm and quiet words that your still ringing ears need a moment to register them. “How can I do anything less for ones who have aided us so well? Just LOOK AT THIS GRAVITON CANNON,” she suddenly booms, and then said weapon abruptly emerges from her chassis, and she wriggles it slightly.

“I thank you, Fabricator-General Theodosia, for your benevolence in this matter.”

“YOU ARE WELCOME!”

As she turns to leave, she then suddenly swings about once more.

“Ah,” she murmurs, which for her equates to somewhere between a shout and a raised voice, “You are aware that my world, long ago, once supplied weapons to the Legions of the Great Crusade?”

“…yes?”

“Your Master of Forges, Martellus, I have heard his charming devotion to the machine whenever he descends to maintain or take with him his for turrets for repair,” she stomps a bit closer almost conspiratorially, “I would grant him something, if he desires it.”

“Oh?” you lean back slightly so that she does not brain you with her various weapon emplacements.

“We have discovered for ourselves two small caches of the noble Rapier mobile weapons platform, amidst the ruins as we went about our repairs. They were,” she growls, “Nearly destroyed when we reached them, and already we have found signs of more advanced things being lost as we advanced together across Aerus, but no matter!”

She looms over you.

“For your service, your aid, and in respect of the one who shows such devotion and care to the machine, I would grant you access to one of them.”

You have honestly never heard of the things, which is certainly a rare occurrence for you. Even so.

“I thank you once more, Fabricator-General,” you bow your head.

“I HAVE BEEN GRANTED MY OWN FORGEWORLD, Chapter Master,” she chortles which sounds extremely disquieting as it emanates from her speakers, “A WAR TO KEEP IT, LIKELY A WAR TO KEEP IT PURE. WHAT MORE COULD A MAGOS PRIME WANT FOR?”

The return to the Omnia Arcanum is a joyful one, to be sure. For five years your brothers have been on Aerus, and now may return to their quarters outside of the aesthetic of Aerus. They may return to consecrated shrines in your ships, receive and confess to the Chaplains in the proper places, and allow their arms and armors to be truly cleaned of the filth and grime put onto them by the constant battling of warp-corrupted machinery and its cybernetic monstrosities. Yet there is no rest for the Angels of Death in any long-term sense, as you sit in your own quarters and slowly go through the information that has been collected for you by your leadership. Training will continue regardless, and does so as the newfound experience upon Aerus is further refined within the minds and hearts of the Blood Ravens.

The Hrud Infestation has been defeated, and the worlds now reclaimed. Many a Magos Biologis and members of the Eccelsiarchy have been called to bless the soil and cleanse it of whatever it is that the Hrud leave behind them as well as lead and assist in reconstruction efforts of the colonies. A moderate amount of that success is due to the sophisticated weaponry that your ships possess in terms of orbital support which certainly outweighed the less accurate and less powerful weaponry possessed by the Imperial Navy which allowed the Guard to advance further and further against the Hrud. That is not to say that things were not fierce in their fighting, of course,

That stalwart man, consistent ally of the Imperium and the Blood Ravens, Colonel Merrick, was aged into almost right into retirement by the vagaries of the Hrud’s monstrous entropic field before finally disappearing on a particularly violent battlefield. Indeed, much of if not all of the 85th​ Vendoland has been rendered dead or useless, all aged to a point of combat ineffectiveness. Many of them have died of now age related causes, such as heart failure or dementia or simply in their sleep, and those that remain are now most certainly retired on the worlds they fought and died to save. Colonel Merrick himself on the other hand, has not been seen since his disappearance as he plunged into the heart of a hrud formation at the biological age of 107 years old while carrying an entire heavy bolter by himself against them.

He will be remembered. For all that he was no space marine, he fought with skill and strength against forces that would have destroyed lesser men and indeed slew many a space marine besides. Against Chaos, against the Tyranids, against Orks and Eldar as well, he fought and lived. There are still stories told by Tarkus and Cyrus of the brave Imperials who braved the heart of the tyranid-held territories of Tython Primaris alongside them. That they were brought down by the Hrud, so many to but age and time rather than battle itself is enough to bring a rage even to you, for such an ignoble end to so many noble men and women. It is that, surely, the threat of dying in a bed with the skin sagging off of him, which drove Merrick to lead that charge despite the effects upon him it would surely cause. That his final charge broke the Hrud and sent them into retreat is but one more accolade to surely be heaped upon him. As a gift from the last of the 'Crazy Riders' who were still in possession of their senses and not so old as to be entrapped within abodes for those of their advanced age, did they in what is perhaps an almost insane amount of fatalistic devil-may-care secrecy gift unto your Chapter some of their secretly looted weaponry of the Hrud, a handful of the mysterious and highly prized Hrud Fusils, which would have if sold to those who slaver over them such as the Officio Assassinorum and the Inquisition surely provided an extremely wealthy retirement for the entire now retired regiment.

As for Vandis, that treacherous blight upon your Chapter, thief of your relics, and likely secret aid to Kyras in his time of corrupting the Chapter, Cyrus sets out with an almost feral eagerness to slay him. The 10th​ Company, accompanied by a small number of others, slipped into the Subsector Carios where the latest signs of his presence had been found. A largely unexplored area of space, your brothers act not only as hunters but almost as cartographers of all three of the star systems within the Subsector. Within the Pharos system, they find a series of mostly dead worlds, where no life ever evolved. Yet cautious is Cyrus, that he endeavored to investigate them to ensure that they are not heralds of, say, necrons hiding beneath. To his credit, he scoured the system, and while he found the faintest of trails of ships that had passed through, none were of human design. Rather that of orks, in truth, as well as others that were not readily identifiable. Already, though, squabbling has begun over who gets the rights to the mineral wealth present on those worlds, or at least prospecting, between the Mechanicus and the various Imperial powers within Sector Korianis.

It was in the Faiyuum system that near-disaster struck. The 10th​ Company was flying through the quiet star system, noting a Feral World with a reasonable human populace for others as well as a gas giant and other such things, when they were attacked from nowhere. Eldar corsairs, bearing the black edges and unique weaponry used by those known to you as the Dark Eldar, the 10th​ Company lost two of its escort ships before Cyrus was able to locate the actual location of the attacking enemy vessel and boarded it with the whole of the 10th​ Company. Some were lost, others were not, as they fought in the shadows and holo-fields natural to the infuriating xenos. In the end, however, he was successful, if only after another frigate was brought down with all hands lost. The 10th​ Company refused to cease their search, however, though they then played a deadly game of cat and mouse with other such vessels throughout the star system. Apparently, they have been using the unnamed Feral World below as their own personal playground of terror and pain to feed off of while holding an anchorage in a system that the Imperium has let be for far too long. But the recently repaired strike cruiser The Howling Hatred managed to win the fight, scattering the other ships before it and allowing Cyrus to make a tactical retreat for then.

Leaving those battles for later, and they will come, he then found himself within the Thuum System where he finally found more concrete signs of Vandis. The man himself is nowhere to be seen, but Cyrus scouted out a large mansion and fortress in one upon one of the habitable moons orbiting the single planet - a gas giant - within the system. Upon raiding it, he found much corrupted personnel who had been apparently left abandoned by their master years before, with Chaos symbols liberally covering the place. Brutal yet informative interrogations have revealed even more information. Vandis fled to this secret ‘home away from home’ where he slowly built up his forces to return to retake Aurelia, only to be beaten back once again. From here, in Thuum, he assembled his ships, and fled fearing reprisal almost immediately afterwards, fleeing not into Segmentum Obscurus, as you might have suspected of such a tainted wretch but instead towards the greater untamed wilderness of Segmentum Ultima. Cyrus elected, considering the years that had passed, not to pursue further without further speaking discussion with the rest of the chapter.

On a somewhat brighter note regarding the heresy of his claiming Blood Raven relics for his own, is that some were even recovered by Cyrus from his compound within the Thuum System. Just as last time, and perhaps even more disturbingly, is that they were Terminator armor suits and weapons with them. That he possessed such equipment, so rare and honored, is deeply concerning to you. For more was yet taken from his vaults as he fled, this you know. Was he determined to lord over these things, or was he daring to sell them unto others? Ever suit is a treasure, and the thought of him using them in such a manner is enraging to you in the extreme. Regardless, Cyrus returns, the 10th​ Company with him. In an interesting display of his own authority, he has seen fit to speak with Tarkus and have Kaius Jacobus declared a sergeant, his own prowess elevating him despite not possessing decades more experience. Such is his abilities thus far, they say.

But now Cyrus has returned, as has Hadrian, and the Chapter stands united…and victorious! You shall allow the younger members to celebrate, as they should, before you once more plunge into the fires of war. For you have heard the scattered messages from around the Imperium. Armageddon fallen, or perhaps only just about to. Deliverance itself under siege. The increasing amount of daemons and ships which flow from the Eye of Terror battling the forces of Celestine’s Crusade. Orks and Necron and more rising in greater number, all the while the Third Tyrannic War continues to rage across the galaxy, in Tempestus and the southern reaches of Ultima. The Mechanicus reporting to you that they believe they have finally located Kaptin Bluddflag in the depths of the Judgement of Carrion with the last of his followers. You are but one Chapter amidst all of this, but you know your duty. You will slay the enemies of the Imperium and protect its peoples until your last breath. That is your duty, given unto you by the Emperor.

And He shall not find you wanting.
