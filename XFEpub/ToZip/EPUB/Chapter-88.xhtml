Once, in long ages lost, the space marines plied the stars in vast legions. They assaulted not worlds, but entire civilizations at once. Not kings, but kingdoms in their multitudes. Not warbands, but armies beyond count. For all that those days are long past, for good or ill, there remains yet the ancient imperative and skill honed to the finest edge in the galaxy. Planetary assault, not the battles of the void, is what the space marines, the Adeptus Astartes were built for. No longer do they march in their hundreds and thousands, a great and terrible hammer and anvil in one unified form. But still the Codex provides, at least somewhat, for what must be done when it must be done. Above the world whose name would be unpronouncable to human throats - not that they would ever care to - which had been obscured for so long beneath a vast storm, crimson and black wove together into the shape of doom. The striker cruisers were one thing, yes, but they were not battle barges. Shells were loaded, cannons were primed, and the fleets of three entire companies slowly drifted into position. All the while, great tears in the storm lattice exposed the dark world below to the sunlight once more before rapidly healing once more. One tear remained widest and unhealing, a weeping sore on what had previously been an elegant if horrific defense in construction and continuation.

One of the greater spawn disappeared mid-charge, to terrified screams and howls. The change was immediate. Many of the larger creatures whose biology had only been possible with the powers of the warp infusing them...disappeared. Or died screaming as they were suddenly unsupported by their own malformed bodies. Then some of their weapons stopped working, belches of warpstuff no longer blasting into the barely holding psychic shields of the librarians. As one creature, still determined to fight and die for their damned God, leapt for you and was smote down by Godsplitter, the skies opened further. The storm itself began to tear and rip free from its moors, as its long and tortured existence flickered.

And then it tore open completely, down the middle, two towers crumbling to dust in the distance where you could not see or hear.

It was enough, the Librarians would later note in their studies, to break the entire lattice. It was a self-supporting structure, self-propagating. It rejuvenated, for lack of a better word, those slaughra and mutants who had lived in the shadow of it for so long. But it was reliant on them, their sacrifices and works to keep the towers functioning and stable. When enough of the towers were knocked out, burnt away, the entire structure failed, for what remained was increasingly over stressed by what had occurred and was unable to keep up the now vastly increased upkeep. Thus, when another tower was burnt out by the demands placed upon it, so followed another, then another, and another. A cascade of failures, each with spectacular explosions and collapses unique to each tower. The worldstorm tore itself apart, as the sorcery which had crafted it died out. You, of course, saw none of this.

What you saw was a world's xenos populace dying. They died fighting, which on some level might be admired, but they died all the same. Their greatest were slain as the laws of reality asserted themselves once more. Their lessers were overtaken by a storm not of lightning, but of bolter shells and lasfire. Of lascannons and krak missiles. Some ran away, tried to flee, once they realized the truth of things. Perhaps to try and hide, perhaps they were simply so afraid of these giants who had come to their world and brought such total ruination that they fled without thinking at all. It didn't matter. The landspeeders ran them down, while thunderhawks corralled others. To be certain, there remained yet entire armies worth of slaughra and mutants upon the world, but while some could still manifest their powers, their greatest sites had been broken before them.
