On Vengeful Wings
+070.M42+ To +075.M42+
I am a Blood Raven, born into the starry sky on wings of hate. Hate for the alien and his opposition to Man. Hate for the treason of brothers and fathers. – Reclusiarch Mikelus​

The Saharduin were a monstrous alien race who had languished under the yoke of the Imperium for thousands of years. First, it had been the Space Wolves, who had faced their Piscean Warriors onboard their ships, on the land, and in the waters of their preferred worlds. But before the watery domain of the aliens could be fully scoured, the demands of the Great Crusade had called the Space Wolves away. The fires of the Horus Heresy had ensured the Saharduin were transformed into little more than a splintered, bloody footnote in the annals of one of the first Legions and the Imperium. It had taken over a millennium for the Saharduin to claw their way back, and yet the Imperium had broken them again, and again, and again. Fate had intervened every time, either in the form of bureaucratic competition, alien incursions, traitors, heretics, and various other stella phenomena. In the end, the Saharduin Swells, as the xenos termed their domain, survived. They were, as such, well documented. The ‘crash rifles’ they bore were somewhat lesser peers to the esteemed bolter, while in melee they preferred their teeth, their claws, their knives, with leaders bearing power weaponry. They preferred to raid their enemies at the flanks, but were not afraid of full-frontal combat, boarding and emptying out ships and utilizing their captured prizes as scrap and salvage. Their religion was a primitive animistic thing, and psykers amongst their kind were vanishingly rare. Such were the notations in the records of the Librarium. They had not previously been known to be corrupted by the powers of Chaos, but it ever seemed in these dark days that the Ruinous Powers were waxing in strength and reach.

“Boarding pods loaded and ready, Chapter Master,” the serf’s voice crackled across the vox.

It had been a frantic, swift thing, the Imperial Navy troop transports practically redlining their engines to fill the ships of the Blood Ravens with void armsmen, hard bitten men and women used to a life of either boarding other ships or receiving boarders, and little else. They had been both awed and struck dumb by the haunting emptiness of the Litany of Fury and the other strike cruisers. Filled and made pristine by servitors and serfs, records, annals, trophies, symbols of triumph and loyalty filled the well-kept halls. A Battle Barge was meant not just for the carrying of up to three companies, their specialists, and their vehicles pools, but a veritable army of serfs and servitors as well. Here, now, the armsmen of the Imperial Navy far outweighed all of these things. A more prideful Chapter might have refused, the indignity of their weakness being shown to all and sundry too much to bear. But the Blood Ravens had been flayed open before the eyes of the entire Imperium, their weakness and the treachery of Kyras known to allies and enemies alike.

It did not mean that Gabriel did not bow his head in shame as the last of the void armsmen who could be transferred before contact was shuffled into the many, many boarding torpedoes which up until this moment had only ever been used by Blood Ravens. That they had been brought to this point by Chaos. By the tyranids. By the Eldar. By Boreale. His own weakness which had left him reduced in such a state, Apollo in suspended animation, only added to it all. A cloying, choking tide of shame washed over him, his gorge rising, but he forced it back down as his head rose. Klaxons began to ring out across the ship. The Blood Ravens would be the tip of the spear, the ships of the Imperial Navy remaining behind to use their lances at long range. For the first time too, Sword of Reckoning, the personal ship of Inquisitor Adrastia, would be able to fully commit itself to a battle in the void, rather than the stoppered progress of entry into the system and the mad dash over Opus Maxima.

“Inquisitor?” Admiral Graves’ voice was almost drawling as the shared battlefield command channel of the vox crackled open.

“What is it, Admiral?” Adrastia snapped back, astonishingly still conscious despite her own estimation of her wounds.

“I couldn’t help but notice the unfurling of your gun ports for the first time,” the Admiral said, clearly bemused. “Very sleek, very nice.”

“Do you have a point, Admiral Graves?” Adrastia sighed. “We are already closing with the xenos.”

“Nothing untoward. I was simply going to remark that the last time I had seen such a set of plasma macro-batteries, it was on an absolutely ancient Retribution-class Battleship,” Graves said blandly. “And that I am glad to see such things again turned towards the enemy.”

“Mmm,” Adrastia replied noncommittally.

“This is not the time,” Gabriel cut in.

“Fair enough,” Graves said.

Engines flared, shields emitters aligned, and generators thrummed.

“Onwards, then,” Gabriel said to himself as the lance weapons began to fire on both side at extreme range. “Into the maelstrom of battle!”

Violent Approach: 70 vs. 72

The concentration of enemy fire would have been crippling to the already damaged vessels of the Imperial Navy. But the Saharduin were not firing on those ships. Instead, they concentrated their fire upon the Blood Ravens. For it was they, in their strike cruisers and battle barge, which were cast shadows from millennia ago. They were not the Space Wolves, but they did not need to be. All such beings known as the Adeptus Astartes were reviled by the whole of the Saharduin race, for it was the boom of bolters and battle cries of power armored space marines which had heralded the destruction of their civilization when it had been at its utter pinnacle. Yet for all of that, despite managing to strip the shields from the Litany of Fury with their utter hatred, these Saharduin, corrupted by Chaos as they’d ranged far from home in search of the whispers that haunted their dreams, had never actually faced space marines in battle.

Suffering only minor hull damage, the venerable superstructure held strong.

And then it was time.

Boardings: 75 vs. 54

Shuddering spread throughout the battle barge as it launched its boarding pods. Aside from orbital bombardment, it was this that the ship had been made for. The Litany of Fury lumbered forward through the incoming swarm of Saharduin ships, which in turn were forced to break off, to twist about, to avoid being struck head-on. Such desperate maneuvers were expected, and boarding torpedo calculations realigned within seconds with the aid of the advanced cogitators of the battle barge. At extreme range, they fired out, slamming into the revealed bellies of a great many of the ships. As the battle barge roared through, its few remaining weapon arrays firing, it was followed behind by the strike cruisers, who themselves formed a spread out trident, tipping and rolling from side to side so that both sides of the launch bays could be utilized, ensuring that just that many more boarding torpedoes could impact deep into the Saharduin ships. The xeno ships had their own point defenses, but due to having to alter their flight paths so significantly, only a small handful of torpedoes were knocked off course, and none were actually destroyed. The armsmen had packed the assault rams and boarding torpedoes far in excess of how many space marines could have fit in them.

Within less than sixty seconds, literal thousands of veteran armsmen of Battlefleet Korianis had entered the ships of the Saharduin and begun killing the enemy. They had faced ork pirates in close quarters, had stormed the mirror maze passages of Eldar ships, set flames to the disgusting innards of tyranid bio-ships, and had fought meter by meter through ships taken by heretical forces. The damp ships of the Saharduin were new to them all, though. At the same time, those dark watery passages were, in fact, incredibly similar to fighting in zero-g combat, as many had done so many times before. Spread amongst many of them, spots of red or blue amidst the green and grey uniforms and flak armor, were the Blood Ravens. The scions of the Librarium were met with universal uneasiness and distrust, as they were all that most terrible of things, psykers, yet whether Epistolary or Lexicani they weathered that enmity as they ever had. More fearsome than that, however, were the legendary terminators, power fists crackling and assault cannons or storm bolters fully loaded.

The Saharduin answered immediately. Many of the boarding torpedoes had landed directly in crew compartments. Others had landed precisely where the xeno design philosophy would place passages which led directly to vital systems. The aliens had remained as static in such things as the Adeptus Mechanicus themselves when it came to their own hallowed designs. This would aid in their undoing. Bayonets and combat knives flashed in the darkness, bellowed cries to the God-Emperor being met with the hissing shrieks and gleaming fangs and blades of the Saharduin. The hiss-crack and steady staccato of lasguns and autoguns found themselves matched by the whizzing booms of crash rifles. Amongst all of which was the thunder of bolter shells and rapid-pace hurricanes of assault cannons. Occasionally, too, did reality twist and rip before the powers of the Librarians.

But neither did the Saharduin ships cease their movement, though they were certainly now heavily focused on ending their foes at close range, turning what had been a swarming charge into a chaotic cloud.

Piscean Warriors that had long given up their souls to Khorne, many of them outright forgoing crash rifles entirely in favor of their blades and mutated fangs and claws. Reality, reasserting itself. He was not fully recovered from his failure over Opus Maxima, his soul, and its defenses, were still fragile. The rents and tears the daemons had inflicted on him were still raw, despite his flesh being mended. As it was, none of the Saharduin present appeared to possess any psykers, let alone sorcerers, and with none of them – even the Reaverlord – possessing the sheer power of Lord Mortirex, they had not been able to stop him as he’d moved through the ship alone. Ship 3: 66 vs. 42

Lucan breathed deeply as he glared at the charred remains of the Saharduin. Though he had long preferred to turn his biomancy skills towards aiding his brethren and his allies, that did not mean he was a failure in other applications.
