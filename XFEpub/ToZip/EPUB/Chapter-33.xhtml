Shedding Blood
+007.M42+
+Honor The Imperium+​

“It is…disgusting,” Justinius finally grunts out as he slowly rises from the ritual circle, surrounded by the pitifully few numbers of the once many fierce Librarians that your Chapter once boasted. “Daemons of spite and fury have infected its very bones down to the steel and foundations, even the electricity that courses through its veins are tainted.”

The Librarius of the Omnis Arcanum was once the grandest in the Chapter, and you suppose it still might, but there is the lingering suspicion of pitfalls left behind in both the real and unreal by its long association with Kyras. As it is, your Chief Librarian has gathered almost all of the Librarians left to the Chapter to assist him in scrying into the surface of a world blanketed in Chaotic energies where the stark and cool presence of the Adeptus Mechanicus remains.

“But we see a path,” the newest Codicier speaks up from where he remains kneeling, hands pressed against the deck plates. “Both for us and the first company to push through. The parts of the world that are uncleansed, some of it is corrupted by things simply mundane. Fierce enough to fight off encroaching metal beasts with minds they should never have had, but not so much for the mailed fist of the Emperor’s fury.”

“Nero is correct,” Justinius nods in the direction of the blind yet seeing Librarian. “It will nonetheless be hard, and the very nature of this world’s tainted territories make it difficult to pierce the veil perfectly.”

“It will have to be enough,” you say as you turn towards the doors. “We shall return to the planet post-haste, then to join up with Captain Marcus.”

=========================
​
“Burn you mongrel creatures! BURN!” you hear the telltale voice of Jacobus bellowing as the marines of the 5th​ company spew fire of literal, melta, and bolter varieties down the throats of the charging beasts.

There are too many to even properly describe, so varied are the mutants that burst from the metallic wilderness all around you. From ancient sewer pipes that have become filth choked dens for these nesting beasts they erupt from all around you, only to run up against the steely fury of the Blood Ravens. Few even get close enough to be granted the dubious honor of melee combat against the Angels of Death, and of those even fewer survive contact with yourself, Apollo, or others who bear weapons for such work rather than the noble bolter.

From up ahead, the bellowing and yet comforting hate-filled voice of Ares Actus booms out as he smashes a creature that looks to be a mix between ancient terran ursine and bovine put together with far too many horns and legs. The 1st​ Company rallies around he and their Captain both as they push forward through organic muck mixing with the rusting floor of the Forgeworld’s ancient pockmarked skin. To be sure, the blasting beams of searing light that erupt from the Litany of Purgation do even more damage to the creatures, but that does not stop you from seeing one of the newest members of the 5th​ Company, arriving from the ministrations of Cyrus, from being leapt upon by a simply massive pile of smaller creatures that only come up to a knee individually.

Roaring defiance, you push forward, smashing away the creatures with each blow of Godsplitter, but by the time you have cleared them out the marine is most certainly mortally wounded. As if summoned by some unseen signal, the gore slathered Constantius appears from out of the melee of combat, the white of his armor almost invisible beneath it all. There are no words to be said, save that the Apothecary clucks his tongue once in annoyance as he begins to go about his own surgical ministrations. You are turning away to guard over him even as his saw begins to whir and the shunting hiss of his sacred device activates.

“Will he live?” you grunt as you batter into a nondescript thing with armor plates that could have been a combat shield on their own.

“In a dreadnought shell, yes,” the Apothecary of the 1st​ Company replies tersely, “But his wounds are too severe otherwise, not with his spinal cord and the brain’s tissues…,” he mutters something that you cannot hear beneath his breath, “Went in straight through the eyes, both of them…into the….no, I am sorry Chapter Master, but these are his last moments outside of a shell.”

“Damnation,” you roar before pushing forward and utilizing the incredible strength granted by the terminator armor you wear to simply lift up one of the creatures and slam it down so that you can stomp it to death.

It is then that your comm bead crackles.

“Chapter Master, the daemonforge is up ahead! We are moving towards it, can you guard our rear and flanks!?” Marcus calls out, the sounds of battle echoing now twice as loud in your head.

“Of course, Captain. Good hunting,” you call back before returning to your own work.

All around you, ‘The Fated’ fight gloriously, and all the same slowly begin to pull back to form a static defense. Why Boreale, your own student, would fail to accept the utility of such things, is beyond you. Yet the anger and shame at what your failure as a teacher, in creating such a student, is a cold one by now. You cannot allow such doubt to cloud your mind, and so put it aside so that you can continue to fight alongside your brothers.

“You…shall…all…die!” a more welcome voice intrudes upon your morose thoughts, focusing you just as well.

Davian Thule’s mind was worried to be forever clouded by the toxins of the tyranids. But in the years since the purge it had slowly recovered more and more as time went on. By now, fighting alongside the rest of the 5th​ Company as they formed up into a loose shield against the enraged creatures that stomped, twisted, slithered, and bounced out to face you. It is such a sight that heartens you further as he uses his great bulk to shift such that it blocks a series of acid-dripping spines launched by some snake-like creature or another before it screams as it is set aflame by the relic flamer born by Kaius. Indeed, perhaps ironically, the stretch of Aerus that you worked to push through in order to reach the daemonforge at all had managed to fight off the more mechanical denizens of this place, but you crashed through them in a tide of metal that would do even the Iron Hand proud.

A land raider, the vindicator, your razorbacks and two predators, as well as far more assault bikes. Though you cannot ride atop one, many more can, able to weave in and out of danger as they unleash the righteous cleansing death that you have brought to this place. All is not perfect, of course. As one small formation of the assault bike ride past you, some bearing members of the 1st​ Company and others of the 5th​ Company, a twin-tailed scorpion creature pushes to the fore amongst the more porcine squealing creatures ahead of it. With a stab forward that defies natural evolution with its speed, one of the assault bikes is stabbed through the middle and tearing into the rider’s leg at the same time – piercing so deep as to hit the second as well through the bike itself. With a grunt, you watch – busied with a trio of charging primate-esque monsters almost reminding you of jakaero – as the marine instead merely roars with fury and a cry to the Emperor before stabbing his combat knife into the tail itself, letting it drag him up into the air and towards it as it pulls bike and prey towards its gullet.

An instant passes, as you sweep your hammer forward to obliterate the chest of another creature, and when you look back again the now legless marine has hacked off the impaled appendages and falls forward into its chittering mouth, a bandolier of grenades held in one hand. It is to your surprise, then, when he twists so that only said arm is delivered into its mouth. The resulting explosion shreds both the head and thorax of the creature and sends the marine flying now sans that same arm into a heap nearby, landing atop another pile of scrap, a broken shard of some sort of metal stabbing up and through his ceramite armor to impale him through the stomach.

Then he sits up, grasps the metal spike, and snaps it.

“APOTHECARY!” the marine bellows, more anger than pain in his voice.

Up ahead, there is the sound of a daemonic bellow which echoes so far back that you can hear it, as well as the responding scream of Marcus and all those who he leads. Another blink, another slain beast, bolters booming all around you, and Constantius has returned to your sight, at the side of the marine who you find yourself observing with a fraction of your attention while the rest remains concerned with the battle at hand.

“Brother Ateren,” the apothecary greets, “How may I-,”

“SEAL MY WOUNDS! THE EMPEROR’S FURY REQUIRES YET MORE DELIVERING UNTO THE FOE!”

With but one limb remaining, Ateren is thus dragged into the line, holding a bolter in one hand as he hops forward to find a better position amongst those that bear combat shields and bolters in a quasi-shield wall to prevent more of the enraged abominations from charging into the rear of Marcus’ task force. All the while he screams utter obscenities towards the enemy, besmirching their honor, existence, lack of souls, and appearance in one almost ceaseless stream of hatred.

In point of fact, he is not the only marine that you have paid attention to throughout this fight. For it is true more often than not that in the crucible of battle that many things can be discerned that more ‘peaceful’ observation cannot quite match. For one, this Brother Ateren, of the 1st​ Company. In another moment, a Brother Caesar who angrily yet quickly managed to stabilize one of his brothers from what would surely have been a crippling injury without any aid from Constantius. Of the 5th​ Company, one of the newest recruits from Cyrus demonstrated a bolter of higher than average firing rate, something quietly noted to you by Apollo as he bore the noble Rogal’s Fist to punch his way through the enemy with high speed. A moments interrogation mid-fight during one breath from the next brings you the knowledge that he modified it himself, though he paled at the thought of having committed tech-heresy in doing so. Apparently the boy was once a blacksmith’s apprentice on his homeworld before attending the Blood Trials.

Here, now, upon Aerus, you scour your own Chapter for those vital members for filling ranks beyond that of a battle brother. And here, now, upon Aerus, you have found them. These, along with those that Cyrus tells you of, grants a sliver of hope for the future of the Blood Ravens.

“For the Emperor!” the vox crackles once more, and you feel the earth around you tremble as a great many explosives go off, one after another at the site of the daemonforge.

Smoke stacks that forever plumed out acrid smoke whose color changed daily and by the minute begin to crumple in amongst themselves. While the 5th​ Company held the line, the 1st​ Company plunged deep into the belly of the most certainly sentient beast that was the daemonforge, and from the sheer amount of explosives you can feel still rippling outward, they succeeded in their course to destroy the thing.

Of course, the issue then, is punching your way back towards the Mechanics lines. Luckily, you have already torn to shreds much of the route that you passed through and shall now pass through once again.

The tale of fighting endless waives of mutated and possessed servitors, animalistic machines given unholy life, and more, is one that is proudly enshrined within the annals of the 1st​ Company. At the same time, the noble holding action of the 5th​ Company which allowed their brothers to commit all of their strength at once is held with equal regard. All are proud of the work that was done, even at the injuries and casualties taken by very many in the fight. Of them all, only one was reduced to being interred within a dreadnought, but of that there are still injuries aplenty which remain. Your much reduced apothecarium certainly is in dire need of repopulation, and it is now that you focus your efforts upon it.

While you spar and train, the Chapter along with you, much work is done within the hands of your apothecaries. The greatest implantation of all your gene-seed is done, and you find yourself remarkably calm about the fact that you in doing so have removed the vast majority of your remaining gene-seed from being used for any other purpose. It is a dangerous gamble, and yet at the same time you can take heart in the test-slave cloning chambers that you are making access of. While the gene-seed they produce will be unseasoned, for lack of a better word, you know well that the galaxy will happily provide foes for such things to be dealt with by blood and war.

The remainder are equally put once more into the Test-Slaves, another half a decade to pass before harvesting is possible.

​
Martellus quietly chanted to himself as he began to fit the chassis together, each part of the holy vehicle who he had been bid to construct requiring ritual purification and blessings before being fit together. Yet it was something that he was more than glad to partake in. To craft such a vehicle for the service of the Omnissiah, the Emperor, and truly the Imperium as a whole, was not just a duty, it was an honor. And so he continued to go about his work. While his personal affection for the wondrous protection that a few good turrets could create was strong, he could not afford even the slightest of failure when producing this vehicle. Too much was lost thanks to the traitors, and what could not be recovered would have to be rebuilt or built anew entirely.

And so Martellus went about his holy work, finished with his other orders far sooner than any others might have expected.

“And this shall be the place where the Blood Trials take place,” Cyrus murmured, pressing a few more buttons into the data-slate before placing it back onto its clip at his side. “Begin the construction,” he swept his hands out, and from behind him the servitors and serfs moved forward.

The world was a strong one, tough. Its population was pure, as near as he could tell, though any who came from here would require of course strong testing by the apothecaries. They could not have passed all this time with a world filled with daemons and tainted by the warp within the same solar system without some taint that even he could not see. Across the world, the 10th​ Company had already spread out, testing themselves against its brutal and icy environment while observing the denizens of the planet already for potentials to refill the ranks. Some might have accepted laxer standards, but he would not compromise on them.

Never again.

​
Acquiring information on the nearby Hrud threat is not hard. The Imperial Guard and Navy of the Sector are not quite used to dealing with such a threat in such numbers. Yet they fight well, as far as the reports go. Astropaths carrying discrete communications as you go through what channels you can inform you quite well of the lumbering yet admittedly powerful movements and assaults of the Guard as they land in the infested area. The short lives of thousands of Guardsmen have already been snuffed out not by the uncanny weaponry of the vile xenos but by the strange entropic energies that they carry with them, yet they push on. For a moment you thought that the Blood Ravens might have been required to give aid in some measure or another, but whether by due to the great stain on your honor and reputation that you still carry with you or some other reason, they have instead asked and received the aid of the Order of the Lost Rosetta. With them on the battlefield it is vastly unlikely that your aid would be accepted whatsoever. Nevertheless, the infestation grew strong without much intervention, and if the Imperials and Sisters of Battle should fail or falter, you must be ready to purify the xenos if need be.​
