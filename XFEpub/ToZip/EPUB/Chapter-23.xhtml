Unfurling Wings
+002.M42+
+Faith Is Purest When It Is Unquestioning!+​

The first year of the forty second millennium passes simultaneously fast and slowly. It is agonizing.

Part of you strains to raise up the chapter and make all haste towards Cadia and all the other battlegrounds being fought as the 13th​ Black Crusade continues to pour forth from the Eye. To bring the might of the Blood Ravens against the Black Legion and all the other traitors there to prove yourselves. To strike out at the taint in the galaxy with genuine and full force without the trappings of the corruption that Kyras had slowly slipped into the chapter for so long. The warrior demands battle and it is a struggle to restrain it even as you hungrily search for reports and rumor as to what has and is happening in that distant war. A special iota of attention is kept on any mention of your own chapter and thus Aramus and his Penitents.

Yet the greater part of you knows the duty before you. The Blood Ravens cannot serve the Emperor if they are all dead and dying so soon after your ragged victory and purge. Would it be better to sacrifice the whole of the chapter in cleansing flame or to allow Aramus his penitence in payment for allowing the Blood Ravens to once more grow strong? Before you were a Chapter Master, you were a Captain, and there you well learned the lesson you put into practice here. You are not a young Assault Marine ready to dive into battle heedless of any and all consequences other than the death of the enemy. You have a responsibility to the bigger picture and the chapter to lead them well, which will therefore mean that sometimes that you will not lead them into battles. Wounds must heal, ranks must be refilled.

It is with that thought in mind that you order Cyrus to bring his discerning eye to Meridian once more - . The last marine to come from the Hive World in the past seven centuries was Thaddeus Rex and though the young marine proved himself time and again during the Aurelian Crusades he nonetheless was tainted alongside his commanding officer unlike the others who accompanied Aramus. Even so, after the continued ravages of traitor and xenos upon the world there is perhaps a chance that the populace has lost the softness normally associated with Hive Worlds. Perhaps even then Kyras was involved in ensuring that the planet was not recruited from but that would go into the bounds of nigh Inquisitorial paranoia and you need no such self-created mental pressures.

Cyrus grunted as he looked on dispassionately upon the corpses strewn across the haphazard arena the 10th​ Company had crafted. In the distant past a pair of Space Marines had been present at all times with rotating shifts lasting months over the Meridian Arena. Just as there is an arena on every Recruiting World of the chapter. Unfortunately, the Meridian Arena had been destroyed during the purge, one more sacred place to the Blood Ravens destroyed by the traitors within. As such this new one constructed atop one of the spires of a fully deceased noble family was seeing blood spilt upon it for the first time. He had delved deep into Meridian in search of recruits but the chances of replicating Captain Thule’s meeting with Thaddeus revealing a noble and courageous heart in time of crisis was essentially nil.

Even so.

Five young men gasped for air as they staggered and forced themselves to stay standing, and to Cyrus’s surprise only three of them were from the Underhive. Two of them were of all things the children of some of the Hive’s nobility. Distant, distant children of course, no heirs were present. But for all that they were of so called noble blood Cyrus had shown them no preferential treatment. The noble blood of mortals did not matter to a Space Marine. Blood coated them and he refrained from commenting on how they could have used weapons with greater ease and that some would have likely benefitted from more aggression rather than defensiveness. But they were but Aspirants. If, if they managed to survive the process into becoming Scout Marines, he would speak to them further.

As it was, he gave a single nod of his head and let his better students begin walking forward to take the last of his crop into the waiting Thunderhawks.

For a world that had seen only one successful recruit in seven hundred years, forty total Aspirants were better than he had ever suspected. Perhaps losing a full third of the world’s populace to the ravages of Chaos and xenos had benefitted it more than Cyrus had initially thought.

[Integration: 50/100]​

The First Company is meant to be the highest premier fighting force in the whole of the chapter. The fact that they have not responded entirely with favor towards their comparatively inexperienced Captain is therefore not outside of your expectations. He is to their minds quite untested indeed and there is little that can be done for such a situation other than to let him prove himself or fail in shame. Still, you do what you can to try and mesh the commander and his troops together. In these dark days for the Chapter you simply cannot afford to allow dissension to fester in any shape and form. Your words are taken in, you see this, but whether or not it will sink into their hearts and minds is another question entirely.

At the least, by the end of the year after several observed training exercises the 1st​ Company is at least not disparaging their Captain out loud. Or at least within earshot of you or the Captain himself. It is not a perfect solution, of course. No, that shall only come when Crixus fights in a true battle alongside them.

You are running ever lower on Recruiting Worlds it seems, and thus one of the only possible recourses is to pursue new ones. The only issue of course being that being allowed to recruit from a world is a matter that takes negotiations with the Administratum, Mechanicus, and so on and so forth. This is likely due to how a consistent presence of the Space Marines will paint a large target on said world for any foe willing to tangle with the Emperor’s Finest. Also how this will mean adroit calculus to determine population changes necessary to function with the methods of recruitment, and a great number of purely bureaucratic issues which you grow to hate with a considerable amount of force by the time it is all said and done. Luckily at least you’ve recently shored up your relations with the local Mechanicus establishment.

Two of the worlds you look into are within Subsector Aralakh which has been the bastion of the Order of the Lost Rosetta since before you even joined the Blood Ravens, while the third is Agrax III, a Deathworld within Subsector Krasus. Deathworlds are sure to produce strong potential recruits, and they are also for the most part undesired by anyone else save for perhaps the Imperial Guard. One only needs to look towards Catachan to see the truth of that matter. Of all of these the most controversial request is certainly your cautious question towards recruiting upon Saintsfall, the effective homeworld of the Order of the Lost Rosetta. They only recruit women, however, and so the idea is present that perhaps the other half of the populace may be worthy of induction into the space marines?

The results…are not encouraging. The Administratum dresses up their answers in flowery words and ‘heartfelt’ apologies but the end result is a cold one. Junius, first world you asked of cannot be allocated to you at this time. The reasoning about amounts to ‘because’ but you cannot afford in your fragile relationship with the Empire to truly press things. Saintsfall is another story entirely. The Order of the Lost Rosetta is quite unhappy with your request though at least some of their generally sour mood likely comes from the repairs and final clearing efforts they have been making against the tyranids which struck that world. All on that world are theirs and the Emperors, the men who cannot be recruiting into their Order instead becoming effective support staff, priests, servants, messengers, and so on and so forth. The whole of the world revolves around the Lost Rosetta and that you apparently attempted to infringe on this has set the already volatile tempers of the Sisters to sparking. The last detail of which makes sense – as Saintsfall is where Saint Tevalia died and is thus one of the holiest sites of their Order save for the Burial World for the Saint for whom the Order is based around.

Most stingingly, they ‘happened’ to refer to your request as a more polite version of what was exchanged between the rarely lamented Captain Boreale and his strikes against the Sisters of Battle during the Kaurava campaign. You apparently underestimated the repercussions of Boreale leading the Blood Ravens into battle against both Guard and Sisters.

Finally, Agrax III was refused to you. This was not because of some arbitrary reason but instead politics. That was always more Kyras’s game than yours, honestly. The Guard and Navy do not wish to have their authority and presence compromised by losing out on a world within the heart of the Sector and so have lobbied against your attempts to gain a foothold and therefore discredit them and bring ruination unto the Imperium. Or at least that is how they seem to perceive your request.

“Colonel Merrik,” you smile as you step off the Thunderhawk to greet the man.

He is one of the most highly regarded mortals you think the Blood Ravens have ever found, with universal approval from Cyrus to Apollo. You consider such reports as you flick your gaze upon the man and examine the carapace armor wearing mortal up and down. Hefting a rather heavy looking plasma gun with ease, he appears to have barely changed from how he looked as a sergeant so long ago. Even the green face-paint is still there. Behind him lies the Guard troops he leads, all running through a rather grueling series of obstacles and firing exercises that you even you respectable. For Guardsmen, that is. It seems the faith of your brother’s was not misplaced.

The bionics groan as you continue to sprint along the corridor of the Litany of Fury, your hands flashing in time with the training blade you use to just barely bat away Apollo’s strikes. You fail more than you succeed though part of that is most certainly due to his own incredible skills. It still frustrates you but every increment of speed you force upon your body and the machinery within remains there at the end of it. It is slow progress but it is that not the point of this? You cannot allow yourself to stop, to cease training your body. Not until you are at least as you were before you were almost slain upon Cyrene.

And as you train, so too does your chapter.

Tarkus is one of the most resilient marines you’ve ever seen in all your years. The tales of his refusal to fall no matter the fire levied against him during the Aurelian Crusades whether under his original name or the moniker of The Ancient are legendary amongst the Blood Ravens new and old. It was even remarked in one of the rare reports that are not locked away under a seal of shame by Aramus that he regarded Tarkus as the more durable between them – and that was from a brother who had been struck full on by the Wailing Doom of an Avatar of Khaine and the blade of Ulkair and kept fighting on regardless. His attempts to pass on that innate characteristic are brutal and unkind, yet effective. Beatings and blank bolter rounds are put against the flesh of the marines of the chapter for as Tarkus informs you the only way to be as he is…is to experience what it is to be struck until all natural law demands you fall yet remain standing in the Emperor’s name.

Brother Macharius, as it turns out, is a cold teacher as he calmly cuffs heads with enough force to crack a human skull whenever the shooting is off by a hair of accuracy. He demands excellence from all those you put before him and this even includes the scouts and aspirants as they are dragged to the ranges. The First Company acquits itself well but that is to be expected all things considered.
