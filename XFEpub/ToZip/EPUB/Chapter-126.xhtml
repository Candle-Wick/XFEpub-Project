Though they were not the size of battleships or even necessarily cruisers, their equipment could not be discounted. The thought had come to Tarkus like a bolt of lightning as he’d scrutinized the enemy defenses, the placement of their stations, and the shipyard where the quarry they’d come to chase had hastily ensconced themselves. Immediately, the ships of the Imperial Navy, battered but not yet broken, pushed forward in response not only to the monitors but the rapidly moving ships of the Dark Mechanicus. The vessels of the Blood Ravens, by contrast, rushed past as quickly as they could, for their course would take them directly to the shipyard before any other vessel. Below, at least a dozen massive anti-orbital defense lasers lit up on the auspex, charging themselves to fire at extreme range.

The docking clamps had been slagged, re-enabled and then shut off, and yet a terrible sense of foreboding came upon him. It only got worse as he was joined by Justinius, whose face had suddenly gone white, all the while the tech-priests that had come with them were all screeching in binary, frantically working multiple nearby consoles. All around them, there was fighting, as ever more slaves of the Dark Gods came after them. For the moment, the Black Legion seemed to have backed off, but only slightly. Briefly, he scanned the area and the makeshift barricades that had been put into place before Tarkus had boarded the last of the Carnage-class cruisers and helped force it to remain in place. The last that Justinius had informed him was that he had felt the suffocating presence of Mortirex half-way across the station, even as he drove his traitor brethren after them.

“Chief Librarian, report!” He said before both ducked slightly as a screaming gene-hound with heavy bolters for arms fired wildly at them.

“The shipyard is descending ahead of schedule,” Justinius said promptly.

Then the vox crackled.

“Do you think me a fool, Blood Ravens?” Mortirex’s usual booming roar had become a low, sibilant hiss that nevertheless retained enough volume to blow the eardums of a child into red mulch. “That I cannot see what you are doing, have done? Hah! Little children, hiding behind the skirts of the Dark God’s artifice like a child seeking a stranger’s help!”

A sound of metal creaking and tearing came through the vox.

“You think to use this shipyard as a shield? THEN I WILL TAKE IT FROM YOU!”

Tarkus’ eyes widened as he felt another shipyard-wide rumble as the gravity of Opus Maxima began to tug on it more heavily.

“Enemy commander Mortirex location identified!” A tech-priest called out, oil weeping from beneath the green of his mechanical eyes. “Shipyard command center, location avoided due to heavy defenses and demands of ordered directives!”

“Maneuvering thrusters engaged! Orbital drives no longer preventing gravity well from reacquiring station!” Another stated, mechadendrites wriggling nervously.

“Can we teleport?” Tarkus asked aloud, looking from Librarian to tech-priest and back again.

Neither assault rams nor boarding pods could provide return trips, they were meant to be recovered afterwards or replaced entirely. The goal had been to disable the void shield arrays of the shipyard and then teleport to safety, or to reach a hangar bay and retreat in thunderhawks.

“Shipyard void shields have just completed regeneration cycle and are at full power! Teleport impossible! Warning, shipyard’s descent is removing shielding position from allied vessels,” another tech-priest all but wailed. “Teleportation grows more unsafe with distance!”

That was putting it lightly. The greater the distance, the greater the chance that the subject might outright never return from the Warp. Or, if they did return, not whole or unchanged. It didn't help that the system was tainted with Chaos. Already, Justinius had described how the Warp writhed with daemons here. Teleporting at all would briefly expose all who utilized it to such things. On the other hand, the teleportariums of the Blood Ravens were highly sophisticated, and the aid of the Librarius could hopefully ward off the worst potential side effects of travel.

“Mortirex! What in the name of the Omnissiah do you think you are doing!?” A new voice screeched onto the shipyard’s broadcast systems, this one wholly mechanical and androgynous yet audibly shaking with fury. “That shipyard is-,”

“A liability, Fabricator-General,” Mortirex interrupted with a dark chuckle. “Or are you incapable of calculating the percentage that they would have done so themselves if they had the chance? The moment the shipyard is moved, begin firing on the Imperium's ships with the defense lasers. Salve the wound of the shipyard's loss with a pretty painting in the sky made of blood and metal.”

Mechanized sputtering was the only immediate answer.

“Captain, the command remains yours. What are your orders?” Justinius looked at Tarkus with a raised eyebrow.

The shipyard was hurtling down, and they were trapped on it. The further it moved, the more impossible retrieval could become. the further it moved, the more anti-orbital defense lasers would be able to fire upon the ships of the Imperium, which with every second they were beginning to leave behind. Around them, the skitarii, the gene-hounds, the cultists swarmed. With plasma guns, high powered las rifles, grenades and explosives, they were not equipped poorly at all, and in fact were proving as fanatically willing to die simply to injure a Blood Raven as was expected. More so, even. One way or another, the fleet would have to pull back as the shipyard dipped lower, opening them up to the defense lasers. The troop transports and the Litany especially.

A decision had to be made, and quickly.

[] Run For A Hangar: Retreat, and quickly! Reach a hangar with all haste, and try to land as many thunderhawks as possible to withdraw all troops from the shipyard.
