Cervantes was proving to be...an annoyance. Cyrus growled near silently as he watched a series of basilisk strikes blowing their way along the trench lines. The vox crackled quietly in his bionics as he tapped a finger against them, going on a secure company-wide band.

"Scouts, pull back. There is no point in moving through the shadows if they are to be replaced with craters. Relocate back to secure position tertius-five. We will move on to secondary targets."

The ground trembled, and Cyrus narrowed his eye at the sight of what was pouring out of the heavily disturbed earth. From more than that as well, from the xeno buildings which arched about in all directions, crisscrossing where the biomechanical connectors marked one another. The street was enough for a tank division to drive down, and from the smoke and blood thrown up by the artillery strikes came...exactly what he had suspected would be unleashed. Once enough pressure was put upon the Slaughra, it was inevitable that their masters would unleash themselves. Genestealers screeched as they emerged from the sewer systems like blood from a torn open vein. Bioplasma rockets out of a chitinous cannon wheeled forth, slaughtering a bevy of Guardsmen in one strike. From the buildings of the city around them came more of them, the Guardsmen moving through the buildings forced into immediate close-quarters fighting as the aliens emerged from the shadows. The screams of terror filled the air, and Cyrus watched as the Guardsmen were overwhelmed at their first line, then the second. A group of Leman Russ tanks fired, thundering into the fray, but they took casualties almost immediately. A particularly hulking genestealer leapt atop one, ripping open the top, and leapt within to slaughter the crew in fountains of blood.

"Orders, Captain?"

Cyrus turned to one of the scouts who had approached him before turning towards the enemy once again, a hand held out for silence.

"Squads report on targets."

The vox crackle was near inaudible from the sounds of furious battle as the Guard attempted to regroup against the genestealer horde which was likely erupting across the entire planet.

"Supply Depot burned, no casualties," one scout sergeant returned first, the former 1st Company veteran toneless in his words.

"Farmer fields scorched, no fatalities. Some scouts will need time healing, stealth was compromised by Guard assaults on nearby enemy defensive positions," came another's voice.

"Promethium cache has been given over to Guard hands," a third sergeant replied, causing Cyrus' eye to twitch.

"That was not your mission Tyrus," he grunted, "The fuel stores were to be destroyed."

"I saw an opportunity and took it," the veteran's shrug could practically be heard. "Promethium is promethium, harvested by xeno hands or not. The Guard and Mechanicus we gave it over to were certainly grateful enough. The rest of the base is gone as well, detonation packs deployed successfully."

Cyrus closed his eye and racked his brain on Scout Sergeant Tyrus, flicking through the hundreds who had passed beneath the banner of the 10th Company. Former 1st Company. Former 5th Company. Former...ah. Right. Former 4th Company. Part of Aramus' task force. One of those who did not feel the need to disappear on the Penitent Crusade, citing his lack of presence and input on any of the more controversial decisions made on the Force Commander's part. Dangerously uncaring for authority, yet undeniably effective. A tactical marine who carried a chainsword instead of the common knife, for one. Just one more signal of unorthodox behavior. And for all of this...loyal. Subject to multiple exhaustive tests by the few chaplains the Chapter possessed after the purge. Hmph.

"If this brings harm to the chapter in any way...," he finally murmured.

"Then I won't even see you coming," Tyrus acknowledged before flicking his vox connection off.

"Other squads, report," Cyrus ground out. "The rest of you, with me. We're withdrawing from this fight, and deploying elsewhere."

The Blood Ravens slowly withdrew from the front, their missions accomplished. If the sudden desperation and hungry fury the enemy displayed was at least partially that fault, then so it was and would be. One would be hard tasked to ask the Blood Ravens if they cared for this at all. Even more so to find one to ask. Their presence was marked in the aftermath, in broken fields, shattered supply depots, stolen resources, and vaporized food storage.

It is the greatest gathering of the Librarians of the Blood Ravens since the purge. What a pitiful sight. Where once an entire squad of Epistolaries could have been formed aside from those dedicated to the companies, now there are but Lexicanum and Codiciers. Younger. Weaker. More prone to destruction and devastation. Even with Justinius standing in the center of them, his eyes blazing with power, the whole of his weighty power an anchor for the rest, it is a reduced gathering compared to what you know might once have existed. So many dead from Kyras' manipulations, assassinations, and in the end his blatant assault on their existence in the last days of the Purge. It will take so long to find those with sufficient strength and will to replace the lost, and even though you are no psyker you can tell that you will not even do so from amongst the ranks of those gathered here, not fully.
