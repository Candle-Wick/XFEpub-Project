An Unkindness
+045.M42+ To +050.M42+
+Never Falter, Never Surrender+​

"Please, please, no, I can't...no! No!"

Scouts were to be space marines, some day. It was space marines who held to the creed of knowing no fear. Scouts, one might suppose, could be accepted to be a lesser form in some measure. They were not fully matured. Still, it was a jarring thing to watch one scream and plead out of fear as the rest of his companions were devoured by genestealers. To the scout's credit, they continued to fire with their bolter as they scrabbled backward like some lost child, the psychic pulses emanating from the pulsating bio-form which crackled with psychic power scorching his neural pathways as it sedately floated forward. The entire sector was overrun, the Imperial defenders having retreated and abandoned the entire sector to the alien. Truth be told, it had been a necessary measure, to retreat to more defensible positions where fortifications were not obliterated by swirling sand storms. That did not take away from the fact that the Blood Ravens were not informed, and in fact minor subterfuge amongst the orders being broadcast had contributed to this utter failure of an operation.

All of this, Cyrus watched, before pressing the detonator stub.

The scout, and the rest of his dead squad, were incinerated instantly as the explosives went off. Around the Captain, the other scouts fought ferociously to defend themselves against the coming swarm. Abandoned heavy weaponry was utilized, heavy bolters and lascannons spearing larger creatures instantly while sniper rounds cracked against genestealers attempting to flank. Despite this, Cyrus watched as the newest crater began to be filled in by more and more tyranids pushing forth. Occasionally one would see an original slaughra fighting alongside them, but more often than not the widespread genestealer cult which had pushed itself into becoming the xenos' state religion showed itself more and more as the dominant populace.

"Captain?"

Cyrus looked at Marius dispassionately, the helmeted apothecary still managing to convey a sense of vague disapproval.

"Go, apothecary. Take what can be taken."

The apothecary turned immediately, and the one who had once been an assault terminator of the 1st Company displayed his skills once more. Once, he had battered aside the alien from within a set of terminator armor, shield and hammer in hand. His reduced armor was, as it turned out, a detriment. Twin chainswords gleamed in his hands as he slaughtered a path forward to the lost scouts. In a better situation they would have been saved, but their position had been too far gone, too lost, with no chance for being saved. No other squads had been close enough, and so the cold calculus had been performed without ending in their favor. For their deaths, half a hundred more scouts lived. For their sacrifice, the whole survived. As it ever was, and perhaps in that measure they had died as space marines. It would be what was reflected in the records. Better that, than reduced to gibbering ruin by a zoanthrope.

When Marius returned, ten blackened husks in tow, there was at least some good news. Some of the gene-seed had been retrieved.

Not a total loss, then.

The position held for a half hour more until the last of the explosives and mines were planted, and then the Blood Ravens melted away, in failure and shame. They slew a thousand tyranids in the ensuing series of explosions, but it was barely notable as the Imperial Guard reorganized across the rest of the planet.
