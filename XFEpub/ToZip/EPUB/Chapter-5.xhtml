Famed Members of the Chapter

Chapter Master Gabriel Angelos
Born and raised upon the world Cyrene, Gabriel Angelos distinguished himself amongst his peers even before the Blood Trials were undertaken, and only more so during and afterwards. In time, he would advance from initiate to Captain of the 3rd Company of the Blood Ravens, a truly esteemed position won through sheer excellence and valor without any of the politicking that characterized the Blood Ravens under Azariah Kyras, the previous Chapter Master. When heresy was discovered upon Cyrene, it was he who contacted the Inquisition, showing his dedication to the Emperor above all else, despite the torturous realization that to do so would spell Cyrene's doom by Exterminatus. In the aftermath of that darkest hour, he met and befriended Inquisitor Adrastia of the Ordo Hereticus, a friendship he would later call on in the aftermath of the Second Aurelian Crusade. Gabriel would go on to lead with distinction in in the years afterwards before the Tartarus Campaign, where he would end WAAAAGH!! Orkamungus, battle the Alpha Legion, destroy the foul artifact known as the Maledictum, slay his close friend the Librarian Isador Akios who had fallen to Chaos, battle eldar of the Biel-tan Craftworld before temporarily allying with them, and save a significant portion of the populace despite greenskins and chaos space marines. Once again, his resolve held in circumstances that could have broken lesser men. Later, at the Battle of Rahe's Paradise, he came into contact with the eldar of Craftworld Biel-tan once more, even going so far as to make a rare attempt at trust, but it came largely to naught. In the end, he made the hard but necessary decision to enact Exterminatus once the planet was revealed to be a Necron Tombworld, despite the fact that it was a precious recruiting world of the Blood Ravens. His task done, he headed to Lorn V in response to Imperial distress signals.

In the void over Lorn V, despite accusations of heresy from Captain Ulantis of the 9th Company (who himself had long been corrupted by Kyras), Gabriel followed the Farseer Taldeer of Ulthw√© into the Webway to the hidden Eldar planet Arcadia, the Eldar World of Law, to locate a weapon to use against the Necrons. On Arcadia, the Blood Ravens would come to blows with the infamous Ahriman of the Thousand Sons, who also sought the blade as well as other secret knowledge on the world. It was only the intervention of the mysterious Eldar known as Harlequins that allowed them to repulse the sorcerer and escape with their lives. Once they returned, Farseer Taldeer managed to succeed at destroying the Necrons of Lorn V, disappearing at the battle's end. She would return only a short time later in the Dark Crusade, her actions against the Imperium on Lorn V slating her for destruction by the demands of Segmentum Command, the one in charge of such an expedition being Lukas Alexander, who would come to blows with the Blood Ravens on Kronus. With that done, Gabriel would turn to the Targa System, recently invaded by the Tau in the course of their so-called 'Third Sphere of Expansion'. He would face them on all five planets of that system, the 3rd Company alone proving enough to absolutely crush the Tau and repulse them from the system entirely before the Imperial Guard could be ferried there by the Imperial Navy. No sooner was that done than he turned about to Subsector Aurelia, being the nearest Blood Raven fleet able to assist during the First Aurelian Crusade. Despite the best efforts of the Hive Mind, and the death of many Librarians and Astropaths, he would succeed in rescuing the beleaguered Heroes of Typhon - not yet named as such - as they had begun their final stand after poisoning the Hive Ships and Norn Queens of the Hive Fleet assaulting Subsector Aurelia.

Throughout the Second Aurelian Crusade, Gabriel grew more and more open with his defiance of Kyras, refusing to abandon the Subsector to the Black Legion despite the orders of the traitorous Chief Librarian and Chapter Master. Kyras ordered the Heroes of Typhon to stand down and deploy to the Ferox Rift Crusade, on the southern rim of Segmentum Tempestus. Both Gabriel and Force Commander Aramus flatly refused. Both knew that to do so would be to possibly lose three of their handful of remaining Recruiting Worlds, as well as abandon billions of Imperial Citizens to the depredations of Chaos. The 3rd Company was, however, too large to move as discreetly as the Heroe of Typhon on their sole strike cruiser, and so Gabriel was forced to move more subtly. In the end, however, as Kyras squeezed those unwilling to leave the Subsector through his hand in Apollo Diomedes, he absconded with what war material he could, being subsequently declared a renegade by the Chapter Master and Chief Librarian. Even even then, it was Gabriel who aided Aramus in the final battle with Ulkair and Eliphas on the ruined planet Aurelia, and it was Gabriel who subsequently banished Aramus on Penitent Crusade to the Eye of Terror. For he knew that to compromise the soul of himself and his brothers as Aramus had would be to ensure that Kyras would win. Yet he also believed it was possible for Aramus to find redemption, whether in survival or death in the course of said Penitent Crusade. In the aftermath, Gabriel found that the majority of the 3rd Company had been depleted in the fighting, and so folded in loyal members of the 1st and 4th Company into his forces to supplement. Even so, he knew that he could not defeat Kyras alone. Thus, he called not just upon the remaining Heroes of Typhon but an ally within the Inquisition. In the Third Aurelian Crusade, Gabriel called upon his friend and ally Inquisitor Adrastia of the Ordo Hereticus to investigate the allegations Gabriel levied against Chapter Master Kyras. During the Third Aurelian Crusade, Gabriel coordinated with both Adrastia and Apollo Diomedes, who had come to admit that there lay rot within the Chapter and was willing to investigate. He would be the first to arrive on blasted and burnt Cyrene, members of the 3rd Company joining him in battling a newly ascended Kyras. Though he fell to the titanic blows of the former space marine turned daemon prince, his life was saved by loyal apothecaries and significant bionic replacement.

After a short period of adjustment, these bionics have proven to further aid him, increasing his combat capabilities and strength. He now stands resolved to rebuild not just the numbers of the Blood Ravens, but their very character in a new era, even if the eternal question of the Unknown Primarch and the words of the thrice damned Eliphas will forever ring in his ears. After the death of Kyras, he has seen to the rebuilding of the Chapter's relations with other Imperial organizations and their numbers, all whilst wielding the increasingly legendary hammer known as Godsplitter.

One of greatest Blood Ravens in recent history, it was Gabriel Angelos who first uncovered the heresy at the heart of the Chapter and it was he who struck the first blows against it. Such was his will and reputation that the twisted Azariah Kyras did not even attempt to corrupt him, instead the fallen Chapter Master attempted to brand Angelos as a renegade and set the Chapter against itself. In this, Kyras succeeded, but he did not expect that those remaining pure members of the Chapter to actually fight back, much less have any chance of success. During the vicious Blood Raven Civil War which occurred just after the Third Aurelian Crusade, it was Gabriel who triumphed and not the corrupted scions of Kyras. A veteran of battle against every single major xeno foe of the Imperium and then some, sworn foe and many-times victory against the forces of Chaos, and now Chapter Master, Gabriel Angelos is known as a stalwart, noble, and nigh-incorruptible hero of the Imperium to numerous worlds and officials across the Imperium. He has fought in every Segmentum, and his battle honors are many. He has faced warbosses and squiggoths, daemons and daemon princes. He is one of the few living members of humanity to ever visit the Black Library and return, facing the infamous sorcerer of the Thousand Sons known as Ahriman. He crushed the Tau in the Targa System. Friend and many times ally to the Inquisition, his position is nearly unassailable politically.

The Imperium's opinion on his Chapter is far less favorable.

Former First Company Captain, Then Honor Guard Captain, Current Chapter Master Bodyguard Apollo Diomedes
The Captain of the Honor Guard and previously the Right Hand to the traitor Kyras, Captain Diomedes has spent his time since the revelation of just who he had served so faithfully for so long alternatively brooding and being filled with absolute fury. Previously one of the most politically involved members of the Chapter, the Captain has in recent times retreated from such things entirely, focusing solely on his combat abilities and protecting Chapter Master Angelos with incredible ferocity.

Still, despite reaching his former position with no small amount of political maneuvering, he is perhaps the best living fighter in the whole of the Chapter, though it is rumored that the exiled Force Commander Aramus could have matched or even eclipsed him. As it is, he reigns supreme on the battlefield while Chapter Master Angelos leads. Apollo Diomedes remains the space marine who once defeated the Chaos Sorcerer Anupharis the Cruel and Warboss Manstompa Megakilla in the same day, who later fought on against and defeated the accursed Kyras. He simply finds no glory in his actions any longer. Once a fiery and prideful champion of the Chapter, Apollo Diomedes is known as a singularly depressive figure to his fellow Blood Ravens, whose demeanor and words can turn even the most aggressive and energetic marine morose. He restricts himself from doing so when not directly approached, and as such has found himself increasingly isolated from his brothers. Yet this is rarely held against him, for he was a blind puppet to Azariah Kyras for over two centuries, and to have the veil ripped from his eyes as it was in the Third Aurelian Crusade nearly broke him. For his close association with Kyras and having been traitor's most powerful personal agent in dealings with the rest of the Imperium and all of its foes including the Inquisition, a great amount of scrutiny has been focused on Diomedes. Such is his shame and acknowledgement of his folly, the former Captain of the Honor Guard has relinquished his position - knowing he could not possibly retain such a position of importance in the Chapter any longer.

Even now, however, it cannot be forgotten that he remains one of the greatest warriors in the Chapter's history. But whether he will rise from the depths of his own self-loathing or burn in a pyre of his own bitter fury remains to be seen.

Tomb-Captain Davian Thule
Once the second most well beloved Blood Raven in the Chapter behind Chapter Master Angelos, Captain Davian Thule was head of the 4th Company for quite some time. With a list of accomplishments stretching quite high indeed, one of the the greatest of his feats was the Dark Crusade on the planet Kronus whence he fought against and found victory against the simultaneous armies of Chaos, Eldar, Necron, Ork, Tau, and more regrettably the Imperial Guard who refused to obey and leave the planet. It is said that during the Dark Crusade he uncovered strange and dark secrets regarding the Chapter and had said records burned into non-existence. Now, he is the only one privy to the secrets of Kronus, refusing to answer even Chapter Master Angelos's questioning. The price for his defiance was being posted for the long-term on the Recruiting Worlds of Sub-Sector Aurelia.

A respected leader by all, he was unfortunately struck down during the arrival of the tyranids in the First Aurelian Crusade with their vile poisons though thankfully he survived long enough to be interred into a holy dreadnought. In the past, his moments of lucidity were few and passing though treasured as a result of the Tyranid toxins which ravaged him most terribly, but over the years steady work by the Apothecarium and time to heal has returned him most fully. He speaks as easily as he ever did, and only by his unshakable will does he stave off the great exhaustion and slow descent many of the entombed suffer as the centuries go on.

Captain of the 10th Company and Master of Recruits Cyrus Octavius
The taciturn and eternally grim Cyrus is now one of the oldest marines in the entire Chapter after the near destruction of it during the Civil War of 749.M41. His deeds are legend, though more of the whispered kind than the boasts of others. This is due to his unusual political and social status in the Chapter. Highly unorthodox, whispers of impiety and worse drove him to prove himself by serving in the Deathwatch for nearly two centuries where his eccentricities were welcomed and fully embraced.

It was only after the battle of Victoria Primus that he ended his tour of duty, returning to his former company which had come under the command of Captain Indrick Boreale. After decades of resistance to his knowledge and expertise, Cyrus finally requested to serve under Captain Davian Thule. Thule was quickly convinced and soon began to implement many of Cyrus's tactics as a matter of course. Fighting alongside Thule in the Dark Crusade of Kronus solidified their eternal bonds of friendship and brotherhood.

More recently, he fought in all three of the Aurelian Crusades, and highly protested the banishment of Force Commander Aramus at the conclusion of the Second. His attachment to his scouts, which has slowly grown to encompass all scouts of the Chapter, is second to none, as is his fury at the now dead Boreale for sacrificing so many as he did in the lamented Kaurava Campaign. This is due to the fact that Cyrus was and still is the premier teacher amongst the Blood Ravens, having participated in the training of Aspirants, Neophytes, and Scout Marines since his return from the Death Watch about a century ago even when he was part of different Companies.

In 997.M41 he was advanced to Captain of the 10th Company and Master of Recruits.

5th Company's Brother-Captain Tarkus Varianus
One of the eldest Blood Ravens alive, Sergeant Tarkus has many centuries of experience. A native of Calderis, he was once second in command to Captain Davian Thule himself, under who's command he gained the reputation of a legendary steely demeanour and confidence in the face of overwhelming odds, as well as being a trustworthy and wise advisor. During the Dark Crusade, Tarkus especially distinguished himself twice, once against the Tau, then against the Necrons. In the former, he faced down a charging Knarloc and Crisis Suit Team simultaneously while holding down a recently established FOB as the Blood Ravens pushed into the city of Asharis (renamed Or'es Tash'n by the Tau). In the second, he willingly sacrificed his left hand and forearm to destroy a Tomb Spyder with a grenade while the detonator to collapse the Thur'Abis Plateau was still being armed in the most critical moment of the battle. It was the latter that gained him Terminator Honors. Later on, he would serve under the infamous Force Commander Aramus during the first two Aurelian Crusades, and there acted ably as a stalwart defender of the Chapter against WAAAAGH! Bonesmasha, an Eldar Warhost led by the Farseer Idranel, and a Hive Fleet of Tyranids. He would personally execute the witch Idranel at the conclusion of the Battle of Angel Forge.

Yet, during the course of the Second Aurelian Crusade, matters took a turn for the worse. While he faithfully served Force Commander Aramus, he increasingly questioned matters as the crusade progressed. The shattering of Angel Gate, the desecration of Daedus' Tomb on Typhon, these were all supposedly practical measures. But the slaughter of a great many Blood Ravens on Calderis when confronting the unwitting Diomedes was one of the most despicable things done, even in the name of the Chapter and the greater good. When Sergeant Avitus, a close ally and friend, was revealed as the traitor amongst the Heroes of Typhon, it was Tarkus who slew him. In the end, with Ulkair defeated, Tarkus was judged pure enough to remain within the Chapter, unlike Aramus, who Tarkus reluctantly spoke against for his actions. Yet, feeling that he had grievously failed the Blood Ravens by not sensing the corruption within Avitus and all else done during the crusade, he donned the helm of the Ancient and took a Vow of Silence as penitence, only breaking it when Captain Diomedes was shattered by the revelation of Kyras's heresy during the Third Aurelian Crusade. Well known and greatly respected amongst the Chapter if not extensively liked on a personal level, he is especially noted for a hatred of Eldar after being tricked and betrayed by them when he was convinced to work with them against a group of orks - losing a number of battle brothers in the process.

In 999.M41 he was advanced to Captain of the 5th Company, also known as The Fated Company.

Forge Master Martellus Ravias
Martellus Ravias was previously an largely unremarkable Senior Techmarine of the Blood Ravens. He had, of course, served for centuries, and had battled xenos and traitor alike across the galaxy as a part of his secondment to various companies of the Chapter. But, as a techmarine, he spent a considerable amount of his time in the forges, building and maintaining equipment for his brothers. All this changed, however, upon his assignment to the command of Force Commander Aramus. Thrust into battle against a massive Splinter Fleet, his true legend began when under a truly tremendous weight of anti-air fire from the tyranids in the final battle of the First Aurelian Crusade his Thunderhawk was struck low on Typhon Primaris. Presumed dead, Martellus in fact lived to crawl away from the crash and discovered he was trapped deep within the jungle filled to the brim with newly feral tyranids and orks. Half dead, almost all equipment shattered beyond repair, he refused to die. Instead, he dragged himself to a powerful Astronomic Array, a device that Commander Aramus had made great use of during the First Aurelian Crusade. Once there, he repaired the device and what of his own equipment he could over the course of a long year while under constant attack, and was subsequently rescued. Following such, he would participate valiantly in the Third Aurelian Crusade. One of the few remaining Techmarines after the Blood Raven Civil War, he was the most senior and most skilled, and as such was made the Master of the Forge.

Force Commander (Disgraced) Aramus Valorun [4th Company] On Penitent Crusade
One of the most famed yet controversial members of the Blood Ravens in living memory, Aramus Valorun's rise from Sergeant to Force Commander has retroactively been forever tainted by the reveal that his promotion was heavily influenced by the traitorous, corrupted, and heretical Azariah Kyras, former Chapter Master and Chief Librarian of the Blood Ravens. Recruited as an initiate from the planet Typhon Primaris, Aramus quickly displayed exceptional abilities as both a warrior and tactician, abilities that were only further displayed when he was promoted to a battle brother of the 4th Company. Participating in numerous campaigns with valor, Aramus was originally assumed by many to have risen to prominence when as a sergeant he gathered together the remnants of three different squads and led them in a daring artillery raid during the Hadrian campaign and returned with zero casualties. Shortly afterwards promoted to Force Commander - barely past a century old making him the youngest ever promoted to such a position - by the order of Kyras, Aramus would go on to successfully defend Subsector Aurelia during the first two Aurelian Crusades. In the first Aurelian Crusade, he would accomplish many staggeringly improbable tasks. With only a bare handful of space marines at his side, he would end a powerful greenskin WAAAAAGH!, personally slaying Warboss Bonesmasha and a number of chieftains across multiple worlds. He would go on to defend Angel Forge on Meridian, aid in the slaying of the eldar witch Idranel, and went on to bring down an Avatar of Khaine - and would do so again during the Second Aurelian Crusade - and stymied the efforts of an Eldar Warhost across three worlds. He reclaimed shrines and manufactorums, and protected ancient archaeotech creations. Against the tyranids, he would lead his small band of Blood Ravens and successfully hold back their invasion before breaking them at the Battle of Typhon with powerful bio-weapons crafted at Angel Forge. Some of the greatest exemplars and abominations from three xenos races fell before his hammer and the weaponry of his forces alike, all within less than a hundred days.

Yet then he faltered.

Some say that it was due to the fact that he was promoted too quickly, raised up without the proper seasoning and experience required to weather the insidious nature. Others say that his victories in the first Aurelian Crusade made him too prideful, blind to the danger. Others whisper that it was only the blatant corruption of Kyras and others that made those who even stumbled upon the edge worthy of punishment and suspicion. None can say for certain, now. What is known, however, is that the return of Eliphas the Inheritor, long-time foe of the Chapter, and Araghast the Pillager, a monstrous Chaos Lord of the Black Legion, spelled the end of Aramus' ascension within the Chapter. Over the course of the second Aurelian Crusade, he would undertake many questionable actions. He broke Angel's Gate to more quickly reach the enemy, he willingly broke open the tomb of an honored fallen brother on Typhon for the wargear within, utilized tainted wargear looted from the enemy, and worst of all, led his forces into direct conflict with members of the Blood Ravens Honor Guard and 1st Company under Apollo Diomedes. Some, likely, were corrupted. But many were not, and so the killing of loyal Blood Ravens - even with the killing of Galan - was almost inexcusable. Failing to stop the corruption of Avitus, another Blood Raven recruited from Typhon, was only one more failure. In the end, even after landing the final blow on Ulkair, it was deemed that Aramus had nearly fallen too far to countenance, yet would be granted the chance of redemption by way of Penitent Crusade by way of the Eye of Terror. He would be followed by a number of his battle brothers who fought alongside him, but not all. Some, such as the Librarian Jonah Orion, Sergeant Tarkus, Sergeant Cyrus, and Captain Davian Thule, would remain, being judged pure. Others, such as Thaddeus Rex, would willingly join him even without being ordered to do so. Even judged as quite possibly the best individual combatant in the whole chapter, possibly only contested by Apollo Diomedes, he was sent away.

Since then, he has been sighted fighting in the 13th Black Crusade in multiple theaters.

Veteran Assault Sergeant (Disgraced) Thaddeus Rex [4th Company] On Penitent Crusade
When he was recruited, Thaddeus was the first initiate to come from Meridian in over seven centuries, the planet in the past having long ceased at providing any viable initiates at all for so long that the Blood Ravens had even ceased to look. However, all that changed during one of the city's many riots under Governor Vandis. At one point, the riots had grown so fierce as to involve the Blood Ravens, and it was there that Captain Davian Thule sighted young Thaddeus holding of crowds of rioters to protect common Imperial citizenry, having become a member of an Underhive Gang out of sheer grit, skill, and luck despite his youth. He was promptly recruited after the disturbance ended and passed through the Blood Trials with remarkable ease, Davian Thule acting as a warmly regarded mentor throughout the process. In time he would quickly find that his true skill and purpose lay as an Assault Marine of the 4th Company under Captain Thule. He would be promoted to Sergeant shortly before the first Aurelian Crusade after only 80 years as a Blood Raven. He would later become known as a startlingly idealistic leader, brash and bold in battle, regularly contributing his squad to battling the greenskins of Typhon Primaris and Calderis.

It was during the Aurelian Crusades, however, where he truly came to prominence under Force Commander Aramus. In said fighting, then-young Thaddeus would contend with the tyranids and many of their most hideous bio-forms, aiding in the slaughter of numerous ork chieftains and the death of Warboss Bonesmasha. It was during the Aurelian Crusades that Thaddeus battled the Eldar for the first time, combating and defeating numerous Eldar warlocks and war machines amidst their own masses of troops.
