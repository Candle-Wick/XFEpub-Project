010.M42 Interlude: From War To War
​
By the very nature of the galaxy, travel in any direction at any time was never fully certain in its success. Time and space were fanciful trappings that did not necessarily involve themselves when it came to the most common form of transport that many of the races of the galaxy possessed. In certain times, and perhaps some of the most horrifying of occasions, those who entered through the warp were not granted leave by its masters. At other times, sometimes as a capricious joke and at other times as simple undirected whims of fate, to travel was to invite arrival before or after one departed. A century after a departure could easily be the result of a simple leap from a star system to its neighbor. Generations lived and died before relief fleets came, the war over long before. Other times ships would arrive from nowhere expecting a fight only to learn that they had come before the threat would even begin to grow.

And yet.

By sheer, bullheaded strength, could such things be put aside. Distance and time ground beneath brutal and unyielding will that demanded and could force such things. By cabals of those who were ‘gifted’ with the touch of energies beyond the understanding of any but their own kind and barely known by those who were, by the refinement of machinery in the madness and inspired intelligence of minds beset by insanity and genius in unbalanced measure. It was by these things that the warp could be traversed and for all the endless tides of daemons and unaligned creatures that fell upon what could not be denied as a feast with fangs to defend itself with it could not be stopped. Stymied, perhaps, but never stopped, not by such things as that.

Octarius. Charadon. The shattered remains of Alsanta left behind from Farsight’s fury. Empires brought to heel, survivors rallied and sent in different directions.

The whole of the WAAAAAGHH!!!! Gorbad. Of an Urgok who was broken upon Ryza whose survivors fled for greener territories to rally. Entire WAAAAGH!!s upon which massive hands had wrapped around throats and bound into servitude with slaughter and noise and pain. It did not matter their goals, their targets, their wishes or whims. All were sublimated beneath might that could not be challenged by even the greatest amongst them. To ask if there was a goal in mind was to gain a cracked skull or even death in return by cuffs that were absentmindedly powerful enough to do so.

And yet.

It was simply impossible for such a mass of life to go ignored. Not when it passed and burned and pillaged its way to the west, driven by visions in the minds of a leader for whom actual madness was still a debatable companion of the mind. Life, glowing with light in many cases, acting as beacons of that tremendous sheer bulk of souls and flesh that happily bounded forth again and again beneath that which led them with bellow and example. For those of humanity, it was something to be feared and then fled from when their meager defenses failed. For those of the eldar, it was a sheer weight that they could not lift for all of their spent strength and was something to be simply avoided while other plots came into being and were enacted. For a dozen and more empires and republics and individual civilizations who had just begun to peek into the bloodied stars, they were death.

In turn, death itself lifted eyes long turned cold. Eyes of crystalline circuitry, veins that steadily pumped with lightning, and dulled hearts consumed by a steady and unrelenting hatred of all things living began to rise from many a dead world. How could death not arise, when iron-shod boots in their billions and billions stomped upon that had once been cities turned tombs in the depths below? Ships which drifted in the black felt energies thrum from reactors and engines left to be cold before the next harvest was to take place, only for hyper-advanced machinery to examine and collate data on a fleet of hulks of utterly reprehensibly chaotic and haphazard design that passed as if like a great river before them.

Such a mass inevitably stretched out, for all that the Arch-Overfiend, the Great Prophet, the Overlord, and whatever other sundry titles its lickspittles offered up, forced them. They were not of all one mind, merely of one general will. Throughout a Segmentum that had been blessed to be free – comparatively at least – of the taint of the darker things of the universe and instead was plagued by renegades and xenos in equal measure, there stretched an ocean of green flesh and muscle. Throughout a Segmentum through which death had stretched its merciless metal fingers, did an ocean divided and spread out so fully find itself abruptly attacked from within and without by what was already dead.

For the Maynarkh Dynasty, most brutal of all the Silent King’s former servants, it was but an extension of their one true purpose: the genocide and systematic extermination of all life. The Imperium had been a good start. But these orks in their trespass would face the full and utter might of all who served Xun'bakyr, until they were less than ash.

So declared the Mother of Oblivion.

There was a flash of green light and the resounding sonic boom of displaced air which sent all of the cyborks present flying in all directions. To be sure, they certainly yelled and screamed, but none of them could actually be heard over the resounding beating heart of not only the machinery all around them but by one single voice which had screamed endlessly for years.

“RAAAAAAAAGGGHHAAAAAAAAAGGGHGHG!!”

Ghazghkull cracked his neck from side to side, and absorbed the resulting hundreds of rounds fired at him from all directions as the defenders and defenses all around him began to fire. For a moment, he opened his great maw to speak, only to blink in surprise to hear that he couldn’t hear his own voice. With great stomps did he stride forward, then, rending apart some most advanced cyborks he had ever seen apart with his power klaw in one hand and the firepower of his own guns. Before him, of respectable size, stood the frame of the maddened one he had come to find. Dozens of wires and cables were stuck not only into the skull but the whole of the body and the spine, and from a throat replaced with machinery after he flesh had been shredded raw by constant screams, did he glare down at the mad master of the hordes he had just tellyported past.

“……,” Ghazghkull bellowed out, at a volume enough to shake the ground all around him, and yet to his bemusement it went unheard.

So he lifted up his power klaw, and cut the Arch-Maniac of Calverna apart to the shock of all those who had managed to stand up after his initial bulling through them.

“Huh. Well, dat was somefing,” he grunted before shifting his bulk around to glare down at them all. “Now den, dat git might have been smart and shiny, but ‘e clearly let dis place make ‘im go crazy. You gits, you like to foight?”

“Uh, yeah,” one of the larger cybork nobs responded, voice unsteady as they looked at the largest ork they’d ever seen.

“Yeah? But ‘ee,” Ghazghkull gestured over his shoulder at the chunks of meat that some tubes and cables still connected to, “Done made ya foight dumb, no direction to ya!”

Then he chuckled.

“You’ze gonna foight gud now.”
