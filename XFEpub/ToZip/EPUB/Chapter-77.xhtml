Gathering Flock
+035.M42+ To +040.M42+
+There Is No Darkness That The Emperor's Light Cannot Burn Away+​

Hestior had barely put down his meager belongings before a wrench was tossed in his direction, though one of his mechadendrites still managed to catch it. He stared at the blazing heat of the forges, dozens of servo-skulls bouncing past with various tools in their attachments. Upon a predator, one techmarine slammed against it again and again, performing the holy percussive rites, while another fiddled with its treads. He didn't know which one of them had thrown the wrench, but he approached regardless. His companion who had been with him at Mars was similarly thrown at, and pointed towards one of the various vehicles strewn across the forge. For a moment he didn't see master Martellus anywhere, until he did. Atop a machine that Hestior only knew through story and pict-captures, the Master of the Forges was stabbing down with each of his five mechadendrites, illuminated by a shower of white hot sparks that almost completely obscured him. All of the techmarines in the forge slowed to a stop on their own work, before finally turning towards a noise that was at first barely audible which soon grew louder and louder. It was something not often heard in the forges of the Blood Ravens.

Martellus laughed, and laughed, and laughed.

"I. HAVE. DONE IT!"

He boomed his victory from atop the rapier, which began to shudder and glow as it activated successfully. A freshly painted Blood Raven was emblazoned just beneath the quad-bolter attachment. Leaping down hard enough to make the ground boom, Martellus raised his fists in victory before turning around so he could face his apparent creation. For a moment he chuckled before turning towards Hestior.

"Brother Hestior! Welcome home. I would have greeted you alongside the Chapter Master but...as you can see, I was somewhat distracted. Gather your tools, we have yet more to do."

In Zemel, the Blood Ravens come, hailed by its colonists as heroes. On Veridius, once known as Greenkrump, the entire might of the Chapter – such as it is – destroys almost any chance for the greenskins to rally at all. Orks are killed in great droves. There are dozens of scout ambushes, tank assaults, and gunship bombing runs which tear apart the disorganized orks. By the end, there are almost no feral orks left alive on the planet, completely eradicating the threat of the Snakebite-aligned orks upon the world. Those of the Goffs are given similar treatment. From the thanks of the Imperials involved, you learn that through your actions the Blood Ravens have leapfrogged colonization completion estimation by several decades. However, tearing apart the entire jungle is something that you have no equipment at all for, much less plowing fields. With the Guard and Order of the Lost Rosetta thusly able to turn themselves to focus more fully upon Pella, Palmyra, and Aigai – formerly known as Dustboom, Spinesnap, and Shinehole.

During this fighting, you also receive information from grateful leadership amongst the Guard about what the plans are for the rest of the Polonius Subsector. The Slaughra are a vile xeno race – as all xeno are – and must be put down. However, there is almost no information on this new section of the Swarm which has sworn to Chaos. With the warp storm dissipating and revealing a new star system, estimates and plans have thoroughly been thrown out the window to make way for new ones. Decades of information gathering had been prepared for what was assumed to be the only Slaughra in existence, but almost no information has been gathered – or can be – on this new section of the Swarm. The Guard made subtle – for them – remarks that such a dangerous task might be something the Blood Ravens could assist with. In addition, there are numerous high priority targets in the section of the Swarm which has been confirmed to be controlled by an extremely widespread genestealer cult. What you do with this information is, of course, entirely up to you. Some amongst the 10th Company are even worth promoting to full battle brother as a result of their experiences, Cyrus tells you. Of less favorable news, however, you receive a remarkably low crop of aspirants from your Recruiting Worlds for some reason. Apparently too many children were above or below the right age to partake in the Blood Trials. Hopefully the next cycle will prove better.

“By the Omnissiah, damn those xenos! Damn them to the depths of cogitator cascades!”

Phokas slams a fist against the wall, only to nearly wretch in disgust at the flaking ork paint which comes off as a result of the physical assault. The Fabricator-General had made it just inside of the reclaimed ship before falling to his knees from the sheer desecration it had suffered. A dozen engineseers disappeared into the depths of the ship as well, while five magos did the same, leaving the Phokas alone with his two dozen servo skulls. Though you suppose he cannot actually retch without having a throat, he did make the noise of a machine equivalent. After another rude noise from his vocoder, he stands, dusting himself off, and strides over to you.

“This vessel has been transformed into an abomination. The modifications obscene. However…”

“However,” you repeat, knowing what is coming.

“Zerann is completely inundated with repair orders have that fiasco of a void battle that occurred in the Polonius Subsector. We are the premier shipyard in Korianis, after all. Aerus might have begun immediately, but of course you came here instead,” Phokas says with pride before pausing.

He scrapes at the ground for a moment with a metallic claw that has replaced his foot, before looking up at you again.

“However. If the Blood Ravens were to grant us some aid in protecting our shipments to and from elsewhere across the Sector throughout this repair process, it would make things more efficient and certain for us. There have been a mixture of orkish pirates and eldar corsairs in the area – striking just after they sent a fleet to Zemel.”

Ah.

“In point of fact, Chapter Master, given the nature of our ongoing relationship, we might wish to speak of a more permanent posting in exchange for diverting our own resources to a mutually appreciable conclusion.”

The translation being the exchange of favors. As it always is.
