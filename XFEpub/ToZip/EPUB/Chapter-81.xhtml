An Unkindness
+045.M42+ To +050.M42+
+Never Falter, Never Surrender+​

On the world once known as Shinehole, you can see the efforts of the Imperium bearing fruit already. A great number of the tunnels have been completely cleared out, fortified, and settled by transplanted folk who shall live and die here as miners. The many valuable mineral resources of this planet shall be put to good use by the Imperium, and here beneath the earth, they shall be well shielded from the deadly radiation that blasts this largely atmosphere-less planet. With the addition of a few cavern-based radiation shields and oxygen production through machinery and plant-life, these people shall be able to bring much bounty indeed. Especially now that you have assisted in the destruction of the last few remnants of the greenskins within the tunnels. They are stubborn xenos, to be sure, but they are completely broken here, and they know it. No more Warbosses rise, and their nobs are smaller and smaller as the fighting goes on, until it is over and done with. Smaller grots and gretchin are hunted down by tracker machines of the Mechanicus, the psychic powers of your librarians and the Guard's own sanctioned psykers, with heavy flamer cleansing over their breeding grounds. When you exit the tunnels you receive further news, that of the world of Palmyra, once known as Spinesnap. The Order of the Lost Rosetta might have burned two thirds of the planetary jungle down, but one cannot really argue with the results. The orks of the world have been fully broken, scattered, and for the most part exterminated.

Zemel may never be utterly and totally free of the ork for generations more, but your work here, as well as that of the Lost Rosetta, Guard, and Navy have ensured that humanity shall have a fair chance here. Which is more than can be said for many.

"Has anyone seen Martellus?"

"Ah...no, Master Calus," one of the newer techmarines shook their head. "We've been working in shifts to properly replicate the forge facilities onto the cleared berths within the-,"

"Yes, yes, I'm aware," Calus waved them away. "That's fine with me."

Covertly, and only after they'd left, did he dare to comm Daedal on a secure line.

"He doesn't know. He's busy with the tarantulas, as you suspected."

In a crackle of static, both techmarines sighed.

"Good. Then he'll be too distracted to know about the-,"

"WHAT IN THE NAME OF THE OMNISSIAH IS THIS?!" Martellus' voice exploded out of Daedal's side of the vox, and sent Calus to wincing at the same time from the feedback.

Elsewhere, in another section of the forges entirely, the Master of the Forges loomed over Daedal. In one hand, he vigorously shook a slightly more artistically designed piece of scout armor, with some whorls upon the pauldrons. Other than that, there was little that was truly notable about the armor. Which was expected when it came to a mis-mixed alloy attempt, and a failed expenditure of circuitry and electrical systems.

"It is..not what you wanted, yes," Daedal slowly spoke as he uncrouched, "But it was the best that could be done. Something went wrong with the heat, a minor tweak to the forges, and...well. Yes."

"This is not going to Cyrus," Martelllus sighed, his fury abating as quickly as it had come, "We will simply have to tell him that there was a mistake, and we will try again some other time."

"You have been most helpful, my friends, most helpful indeed," Theodosia boomed, her massive dreadnought-esque frame shaking with the volume.

Heavy pneumatic steps herald her coming closer, and proffering a highly advanced assault claw, one which Tarkus knew could run with a tremendously advanced power field when activated properly, which was in turn shaken ever so carefully by Captain Thule. The good Captain had truly come to life over the years, the danger and damage caused by the potent Tyranid toxins gradually being excised over the years by the on-board systems and efforts of the forges and apothecarium. Tarkus did not smile at the sight of it, but did feel his hearts gladden at the sight. Despite being reduced, as some might consider, to a dreadnought frame, Captain Thule remained a potent ally and friend. The various tasks that had needed the dread touch of the Blood Ravens across this world were not necessarily the same as Kronus or a hundred other worlds or battles, but Tarkus knew that it would have taken a great number more of skitarii. In exchange for freeing up the resources of Aerus, in turn more power could be put towards the production of the precious Terminator Suits. The fact that Aerus could produce them, but Zerann could not, was a hearty boon indeed. In the past, every suit was to be jealously guarded and exceedingly rarely used. Whenever they were lost, great effort had to be put into reclaiming them. It was unlikely for that to change, but it was besides the point at the moment.

"I wish the Omnissiah's blessings upon you, and to your Librarians as well," the Fabricator-General dipped her chassis slightly in a bow, "With their aid, we were able to isolate a great amount of scrap code and remaining taint and remove it from my world's flesh and forges. Go in peace, and may you bring much war to our enemies!"

"Did you even think about what would happen after you had me killed, Lord Byron?"

Derosa's arched eyebrow had been feared before. But now, now it was far worse, for when she did so before the rest of the court she held there were five silent red and black specters who stood behind it. The threat of House Derosa's own forces were one thing, but far more than Vandis ever had exercised his relationship with Azariah Kyras did Derosa wield the Blood Ravens on Meridian. They were a scalpel against the cults that had been discretely allowed for fun and profit. They were a hammer to the Ganger Games, which had been previously allowed under Vandis to allow betting on the eternal warfare in the depths of the Underhive. Where they walked, the rumors quieted. Corruption - at the level it had been before - would no longer be tolerated. But now, after all this, the Lord Byron, whose family had owned one of the spires for generations, knew he had stepped too far. Every skirting, of course, but now as the rest of the court gasped and delighted in the intrigues, two of the Blood Ravens stomped forth. Derosa herself sat ramrod straight on her throne, one bearing the Imperial Aquila far higher and more proudly than Vandis' ever had, apparently uncaring of the blood which covered her dress or the exposed bone on her left forearm.

"For your crimes, your corruption, your idiocy, and ordering the assassination of the God-Emperor's chosen Governor of this world, I sentence you to the hands of the Blood Ravens you sought to bury in the Underhive."

Byron turned, and got as far as a single foot before his arms had been grabbed hard enough to tear at the skin and muscle.

"No, no, Lady Governor, no, I see now the error of my ways, I was foolish and weak, please!" Byron's cries went very well heard, but not answered as he was dragged away, the rest of the Blood Ravens in the court slowly filtering out afterwards.

More than a few lord and ladies strained their ears, and clapped in delight at the sound of a booming single bolter round just on the edge of their hearing.

The Slaughra engineers exploded, along with the rest of the facility, with no warning given whatsoever. Cyrus slowly rose from beneath the camo-cloak which had been utterly coated with sand, a previously flat patch of the desert giving birth to the Captain of the 10th Company. The scouts, busy watching the tower collapse, spun and fired wildly at his approach, failing to strike him once. The entire squad whirled, searching for the one who had surprised them, only to find Cyrus standing before them, now silhouetted by the collapsing tower. A final burst of alien technosorcery burst out of it, black and purple lightning sparking, before the shield tower fell. A full mile of the desert ground was suddenly bereft of its protection, and with raucous cheers and a deafening blast of the god machines, a pair of warhound titans advanced, sprinting forward to scout the new area of enemy territory. The scouts stared up at him, bionic eye gleaming, before he gave them a single grunt.

"Your situational awareness was lacking. Your explosive placements were...acceptable. Your next task is to infiltrate the enemy territory, and attack any and all supply dumps available. Do not fail the Chapter, scouts."

And then he turned, and headed in. The scouts glanced at one another before scrambling after him. Few of them truly understood the boon that had been granted to them by Trajan Maphar's scrying rituals, but they did not question it. A tower falling here, a supply dump there, mines and detonation packs along this roadway, some nonsensically placed before the enemy would arrive far later, and others part of a greater bulk. The 10th Company followed their leader, and the alien suffered greatly for it. For too long had the Slaughra lived here, on worlds that should rightly be serving the Imperium's needs. The rich resources of the world, just barely tapped by the aliens who had evolved here, compared to elder worlds of the Imperium at least. Even when a full half of their civilization had been cut off by the warp storm a thousand years ago, they had continued to work on their own civilization, barely aware of the Imperium within whose borders they had been born. Worse, when the genestealer cult had taken control, they had worked instead to the dread purposes of the other. Better that they should be killed, in the here and now.
