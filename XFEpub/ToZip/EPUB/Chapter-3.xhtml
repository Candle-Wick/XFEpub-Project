Chapter Characteristics/Deeds/Specialists

Special Blood Raven Gene-Seed Characteristics:
  1. It is unknown why, but larger than average numbers of marines of this Chapter often manifest psyker abilities, many of whom begin doing so after Gene-Seed implantation. Blood Raven Librarians are furthermore more powerful on average than their counterparts in other Chapters alongside their higher than average numbers. This has led to long term widespread mistrust and constant allegations of mutations, corrupted implants, and other things of vitriolic nature towards the Chapter from multiple sources.
  2. Minor Mutation - Catalepsian Node [Stage 6 Implant]: Manifestation of Eidetic Memory. Inability to experience REM sleep.
  3. Other than the above two, the gene-seed of the Blood Ravens is remarkably pure and stable, despite rumors to the otherwise.

Explanation of Various Skills/Characteristics
Represents the capability to connect blows with a melee weapon.

WS-0: Completely Untrained
WS-2: Moderately Drilled, Imperial Guardsman Minimum Standard
WS-4: Hardened Warrior, Space Marine Average
WS-6: Talented Swordsman, Eldar Aspect Warrior
WS-8: Unparalleled Blademaster, Eldar Exarch
WS-10: Legend of the Blade, Gain Legendary Weapon Skill[

At WS-10, a swordsman will forge his own legend, and cut into the pages of history with the edge of his blade. Once a character reaches Rating 10 in Weapon Skill, he will attain Legendary Weapon Skill, and gain a Legendary Perk that will resonate with their style of combat.

Examples:
Commissar Ciaphas Cain, Hero of the Imperium: Reluctant Shield of Liberation: Gain bonuses when on the defensive, ignore penalties to parrying attacks from multiple opponents.

Lord Commander Cervan Dante, The Old Angel: Final Champion of Eternity: Ignore penalties when wielding axes, gain bonuses when dueling enemy champions, gain additional bonuses when dueling monstrous creatures.

Apollo, Forlorn Hero: Bitter Blooded Champion - Automatically hits all Non-Hero units, damage dealt and skill checks receive no malus regardless of injuries sustained. Critical damage increased.)
Represents the capability to accurately shoot a target with projectiles and energy discharges.

BS-0: Completely Untrained
BS-2: Moderately Drilled, Imperial Guard Minimum Standard
BS-4: Well-Drilled Gunner, Space Marine Average
BS-6: Sharpshooter, Imperial Guard Sniper Minimum Standard
BS-8: Master Sniper, Space Marine Designated Marksman
BS-10: Legendary Marksman, Gain Legendary Ballistic Skill

At BS-10, a marksman will carve his own passage into the annals of history, one shot at a time. Once a character reaches Rating 10 in Ballistic Skill, he will attain Legendary Ballistic Skill, and gain a Legendary Perk that will resonate most with their style of shooting.

Examples:
Vindicare Temple Assassin/Watch Commander Vergilius Rios: Accuracy Without Distance: Ignore range penalties with any weapon. With a Sniper Rifle, double maximum range, gain an additional roll for the purposes of confirming a critical hit, inflict additional critical hit damage on Named Characters and instantly kill everything else.

Kasmr Gough: Metal Storm of Death: Ignore recoil penalties with automatic and heavy weapons. With a Heavy Bolter, inflict double the attacks for no penalty. With a Lascannon, gain an additional roll for the purposes of confirming a critical hit. With a Plasma Cannon, double blast radius. With a Multi-Melta, double range. With a Missile Launcher, double range and fire rate.
Represents the amount of force that may be exerted.

S-0: Sickly, Unable to move under own power
S-2: Reasonably Fit, Imperial Guard Minimum Standard
S-4: Strong, Space Marine Minimum Standard
S-6: Very Strong, May Carry Crew-Serve Weaponry Single-Handedly
S-8: Mighty, May Lift Vehicles for Short Periods
S-10: Arms of the Emperor, Gain Legendary Strength

At S-10, a strongman has reached the very pinnacle of strength, and legends of his might shall remain for all time across a hundred worlds. Once a character reaches Rating 10 in Strength, he gains Legendary Strength and the following benefits:

- The character may now operate vehicle-scale weaponry for no penalty
- The character may inflict damage through armored targets even without a Power Weapon
Concerns the amount of punishment that may be taken before unconsciousness and death.

T-0: Sickly, May Die to a Stiff Wind
T-2: Mortal, Imperial Guard Minimum Standard
T-4: Tough, Space Marine Minimum Standard
T-6: Hardened, Feels No Pain From Unarmed Combat
T-8: Bulwark, May Survive Point-Blank Explosions
T-10: Cast From Steel, Gain Legendary Toughness

At T-10, a warrior attains the strength of form that allows them to survive that which could slay two men, forever etching themselves into living memory. Once a character reaches Rating 10 for Toughness, he gains Legendary Toughness and the following benefits:

- Weapons Fire rated below a Boltgun no longer do damage
- Upon suffering fatal wounds not inflicted by a Vortex Weapon, may act for an additional 10d10 Minutes, during which they are impervious to all further injury
Represents the speed and dexterity capable by the character.

A-0: Disabled, unable to move under own power
A-2: Brisk Walk, Imperial Guard Minimum Standard
A-4: Swift as Wind, Space Marine Minimum Standard
A-6: Lightning Speed, Fast and swift, able to sprint without overly loud footsteps
A-8: Thunderbolt, Fast as lightning and quiet as a mouse
A-10: Winged Spectre, Gain Legendary Agility

At A-10, a scout gains speed, grace and control that allows them to slip into nothingness, in doing so paradoxically making their mark as Legend. Once a character reaches Rating 10 for Agility, he gains Legendary Agility and a Legendary Perk that befits their specialisation:

Examples:
Son of Angels: Half of raw Agility applied to Dodge instead of one-third, may apply dodge rating against indirect fire

Ghost of Baal: Perfectly silent when moving unless purposefully moving audibly, may move at full speed without stealth penalties, may seemingly hide in plain sight if one does not obviously clash with the surroundings
Represents education, learning capacity, and one's ability in deductive reasoning.

I-0: Literally an idiot
I-2: Imperial Citizen of upper-mid class
I-4: Educated, Space Marine Minimum Standard
I-6: Teacher, capable of Instruction
I-8: Professor, Expert of a Field
I-10: Luminary Genius, Gain Legendary Intelligence

At I-10, a mind gains foresight and intellect that demands that future generations study the events that lead to the formation of a talent such as theirs. Once a character reaches Rating 10 for Intelligence, he will attain Legendary Intelligence, and gain a Legendary Perk that best suits their field of expertise.

Examples:
Ursarkar E. Creed: Tactical Genius: Never be caught off-guard by an enemy commander, gain a flat bonus to all command actions. Gain additional bonuses when fighting Chaos-related foes, exploiting flanks, and repositioning forces. Once a battle, may perfectly reposition a single unit, regardless of size, logistical footprint or visibility.

Chief Librarians In General: Purview of the Warp: May perfectly call upon any spell known to the Chapter and Legion. Gain bonuses when wielding spells related to one's panoply or legend. Ignore failure occurrence for all spells of Mastery 2 or less.

Most Forgemasters: Liege of the Forge: Automatically succeed at any repair attempts for war machines and wargear in regular use by the Imperium and the Chapter. May attempt to repair ancient Imperial relics, archeotech and esoteric technologies not often deployed by the Adeptus Mechanicus. Relics crafted will be of higher grades than standard.
