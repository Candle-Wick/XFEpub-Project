“We have an accord,” she grunts, offering a hand for you to shake on it. It is a small gesture, and considering the fact that you are going to be soon bereft of twenty good battle brothers stings, but you take heart in the fact that your duty will at least be done in this measure just as well.

And that is that, it turns out. You do not see Claudius himself again, further impressing on you how much control Christina has over not just his own person, but his resources as well. It is not particularly what you wanted to find when you came to this meeting, but the potential for finding Vandis that much faster deeply appeals to you in a visceral sense. On the one hand, perhaps it does make a dark sense that the Ordo Xenos would want to keep an eye on a Rogue Trader Dynasty which has made great riches off of the trafficking of alien technologies and artifacts. On the other, the amount of times you have encountered any member of the Inquisition that have left you or your chapter well can be counted on one hand – and still have most digits left over.

Soon enough you are returning to Subsector Aurelia, and discussing your surprise meeting with the Lord Inquisitor. Cyrus seems ready to burst at the seams at the news, furtive energy coloring his every movement as he drills the recruits that much harder. For a man who spent two centuries within the Deathwatch, to hear that the organization has been reduced to such straits is likely more than a little infuriating for him. The knowledge that the Imperium teeters on a thousand knife edges anew does not particularly please anyone else either. As it seems to be one of the few consistent things about interactions with the Inquisition, few are happy by the end of it at all. In this case, none.

Tarkus is especially aggrieved.

“I respect the Deathwatch, and what they do, but even this alone will set us back badly,” he tells you privately after your meeting with the Chapter Leadership has concluded.

“I am more than aware, Tarkus. But could we do no less?”

“I am not disagreeing with your decision, Angelos,” he shakes his head as you walk through the Omnia Arcanum, “Merely noting that this will cause difficulties. Not least of which that we would be throwing newer battle brothers in the most vicious of battles without nearly enough experience.”

“And yet, dark times call for dark measures,” you respond, finally coming to a halt in front of your own quarters, where much remains yet to be done for the organization and command of the Chapter.

“Aye,” he stares at the ground, his gaze hard, before sighing again. “But I understand. If the Emperor willed it, then the whole of the Chapter would plunge into the fire, unto death…but that does not mean I have to like it.”

“I do not like it either, Tarkus,” you clamp a hand to his shoulder, “The Blood Ravens march upon the brink, constantly, since our failure to discover Kyras’ treachery. The universe is cruel, and unforgiving. And yet…?”

“None shall find us wanting,” you both intone in unison, a small smile quirking its way past Tarkus’ perpetual flat lips, and he leaves you alone in the corridor.

Indeed. You may be hamstrung and bled again and again, but the Blood Ravens yet live.

It is time to go to work. At the least, returning home after a long, long time, comes one of the first marines you ever saw off upon becoming Chapter Master. Quarius Zergan has come back from Mars, and he bears the marks of his trials well. Martellus is highly impressed with the young marine, and puts him to work in the forges almost immediately after his return arrival and the minor celebration it caused.
