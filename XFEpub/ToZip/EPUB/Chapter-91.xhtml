"The techpriests seemed rather unhappy, Martellus."

In fact, they seemed outright upset, but it was considerably difficult to say for certain. None of the adherents of the Machine Cult were those who had faces anymore, so you were stuck analyzing emotionless vocoder-filtered rasps as you had exchanged mutual friendly greetings. For your part, you had no reason to find the strike cruiser unacceptable. They had done their work well, restoring it to full functionality at a remarkable pace. The Machine Spirit of the vessel seemed almost too eager to get back out into the void, or so you had heard them mutter to themselves. Perhaps it was yet affected by the taint of the greenskins...but no, the shipyards of Zerann would not have delivered it back to you if they had not thoroughly cleansed it. No, the Machine Spirit is simply eager to prove itself, and to bring the fight to the foes of the Imperium, surely. As for Martellus himself, he and his own techmarines deemed that the vessel was capable, perhaps even more so, though unfortunately the desecration of the greenskins was such that neither he nor the priests of Zerann could discern its original identity. All that can be known is that some chapter, somewhere, lost this vessel. Which in its own way is something that must be avenged upon the foes of humanity.

"Indeed, Chapter Master. They discovered that, while most of the greenskin 'modifications'," Martellus uses the word as he would 'excrement', "Could be removed, the lance weapon could not without necessitating total disassembly of the vessel - likely irrevocably. While this Strike Cruiser is no longer capable of the same bombardment procedures as other strike cruisers, it nonetheless possesses a powerful weapon for void-combat."

That is indeed somewhat concerning. The techpriests likely found their inability to rectify this change to their holy design frustrating. Worse, the Imperial Navy's concerns of space marine chapters with frigates capable of lance fire worth disputing in their own way. On the other hand, this is a strike cruiser, and you certainly require such a ship. On the mechadendrite, it is a strike cruiser that has been warped by greenskin madness into a ship possessing multiple more weapon arrays, laser cannons, and torpedo launchers. It can still carry a full company, of course, that function was one of the main priorities, but it is most certainly not within the usual bounds of such vessels. It's tactical value, however, should you become involved with more void combat of any kind, is undeniable. A conundrum. One that you will deal with later. For now, the Chapter is largely focused on repairing their wargear and healing up after the many casualties taken in the fight. It was not nearly as bad as it could have been, if your forces had not been amongst the Guard and Lost Rosetta they would have been considerably higher, but you still took many. One of those is Hadrian himself, though your brother-captain is already awake and cognizant. He would likely speak towards keeping the ship as it is, which would not be surprising considering his...nature.

"It functions correctly, though, correct?"

"It does, yes. A welcome addition to the Chapter Fleet. Luckily, the meks aboard did not realize what the forges were. They used it as a...storage room."

He says luckily, but you can feel the fury at the desecration in his voice for a molten moment before it passes.

Then the two of you are silent, simply taking in the sight of the strike cruiser which hangs just outside the Omnis Arcanum. From within the launch bay, it is a stark sight, but as Spiraling Fury drifts past, the differences between the two are obvious. But of course, you would not bring Martellus so far away from his forges just for a simple viewing of the ship that has just been delivered. Especially after the rest of the Chapter Leadership including yourself already saw the thing when it first arrived. Another matter, one of dire importance, has come up as well.

"But you have also received communication on the gene-seed tithe."

"Yes."

Martellus swivels his head to look at you, nodding slightly as he does so.

"They are coming, soon. Possible within the next five years, possibly within ten, warp currents depending."

Would that you could do the reverse, gain back some of the gene-seed you have spent such time dedicating over the years, but you dare not make that request in the here and now. Not with relations being what they are at the moment. Besides which, you gained a considerable amount this year through the test-slaves. Just another matter that must be taken care of. Yet all around you, the galaxy continues to spin. The hunt for Governor Vandis continues through the Inquisitor's agents, farther reaching than yours ever have been, more likely than not. You have received no calls specifically for your Chapter to attend any battle or another, now that Polonius has been reclaimed. But that is not to say that you are not unneeded. Derosa has requested that the brothers seconded to her remains for another year, citing concerns about the nobility of her planet once more as the reason. There are rumors of greenskin pirates and eldar corsairs somewhere in the sector, assaulting outposts and supply routes. There yet remain greenskins within Korianis as well. Potentially, you could even return to the older ways of the Blood Ravens, and send out your fleets into the wider galaxy, to aid where they will. Finally, the missives continue to come from the Deathwatch, ever-asking for more space marines to aid them in their fight.​
