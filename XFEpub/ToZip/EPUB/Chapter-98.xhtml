​
From the warp they emerge, ships practically pristine to the naked eye. Imperious, unyielding, unfeeling, pure embodiment of their creed given form through ship and shape.

"We have come," they broadcast.

"We are here," was the reply.

"Let the tithe be taken, in the name of the Emperor."

And so it is done. Once again, Gabriel was forced to give up more than the normal percentage, a state of matters likely to continue for some time yet. Distant as the Blood Ravens were from Mars upon the very fringe of the Imperium, there would never be as much true regularity to the arrival of the Mechanicus for the tithe. Something that the Chapter, no matter their frustration, could do nothing about. The shadow of Kyras stood strong over every accomplishment and movement of the Blood Ravens, no matter their recent works. At the least it was only fifteen percent. And, of value, was the return of their brothers from across the galaxy, ferried across their ships by the ones they would always have a connection to. Another series of techmarines joined the forges, their training complete. An apothecary joined his brothers in matters of life and death. And so the Chapter continued to strive against the tides of fate which had nearly split it asunder again and again. All this, Gabriel knew, was not yet enough, and yet it was something. The eldar had not yet deigned to make an attempt within the Sector since their obliteration before. But that was no sign of their permanent departure. Nothing truly existed that could disprove the bloodshot glare of the Imperium towards the xenos.

Yet there remain further xenos within the sector. Greenskins, of whom there seemed to be ever more, plagued the shipping lanes in their ramshackle pillaging ships, growing in savagery and number.

The Black Crusade continues to churn, while there are rumors of a new Damocles Crusade to regain the worlds lost to the Tau in the Segmentum Ultima. Then again there are rumors of a Crusade to some point in the Imperium at nearly every moment, though the rumors here grow stronger and stronger. The remains of the Great WAAAAGH!! of Ghazghkull plague great swathes of the Segmentum Tempestus, battling Imperial forces, tyranids, and necron alike. Further east the worlds of Ultramar, where the living-yet-dead Primarch Robute Guilliman remains in stasis, are being surrounded and invaded from every direction by another seemingly endless swarm of the Hive Mind's thralls. More greenskins continue to appear to be heading towards the endless conflict of Armageddon. The Deathwatch continues, throughout all of this, to send desperate missives to every Chapter they can, favors aplenty and ancient contracts writ in blood and honor being called into play.
