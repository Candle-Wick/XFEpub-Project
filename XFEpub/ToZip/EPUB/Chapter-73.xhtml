The Search For Squiggoths
Scouts: 81
Scout Sergeants: 30
Cyrus: 39

“I am impressed, my brothers,” Cyrus’ voice came from just behind where the scouts had positioned themselves in the canopy, causing at least four of the squad to jump slightly. “Where I and your sergeants failed…you succeeded.”

In an admittedly cleverly carved divot into one of the jungle valleys, the grunting noises of the squiggoths were kept from escaping by the unique acoustics and attempts by the orks to keep them gagged. All around them all, the orks hacked away at the rock face, attempting to craft for themselves a new cave or five to hide away their precious warbeasts. By now they had learned the painful lesson of what their endless assaults on the humans of this world had brought them. Most of them enjoyed the fight, it was in their nature, but few enjoyed being slaughtered without the ability to even get close. Something that Cyrus was only too happy to keep from happening. If a scout was forced into melee combat against these orks, then they had failed in their basic training or they had bad luck. Either or both.

“T-thank you captain,” one of the scouts finally replied, flinching slightly as Cyrus turned his scowl towards them. “And…we think the warboss is down there too!”

“What?” Cyrus somehow loomed without moving or coming close to betraying their position, “Where?”

The proffered sniper rifle was offered, and Cyrus glared down the scope to where the largest of all the ork creatures slept. The orkeosaurus had fallen asleep, apparently exhausted after a long march away from its pens without a fight to keep its energy up. But atop it…yes, there he was. After all this time, even without a known name, an ork that could only be the warboss of all of those on Trontiux III stood. Upon the creature’s howdah, it tinkered at its massive stone axe, a primitive version of the sorts of weapons that a more advanced ork might use. A thousand small spines of bone had been driven horizontally through its own back, fluted in fashion as they went from the neck to the base of the spine. By size alone, against all other orks that had been sighted across the planet by the naked eye, auspex, and scans from the ships, this had to be the ork.

“Wait here.”

And then he disappeared.

Explosive Planting: 55

Some of the scouts swore they saw him, in the flash of his camo-cloak, planting explosives about the immediate valley. Thick patrols of orks, discipline created out of fear, marched about endlessly, and with that it was impossible for a full spread to have been dropped. But still, the telltale blink of a few detonation packs and others appeared in visors, Blood Raven codes transmitting to them. As the 10th​ Company slowly filtered in from across the area, surrounding the valley on all sides, the thunderhawks in the skies above sat at the ready. At least once, they thought they saw the warboss rouse himself, sniffing a few times, before he went back to work on sharpening the obsidian chips that had been embedded along his axe’s edge.

Final Planting: 89

From one blink to the next, Cyrus appeared, this time once more in the trees which had become that much fuller of scouts in the meantime. There were no grand proclamations, no warning, no speeches at all. Not even a whisper. The Captain of the 10th​ Company and Master of Recruits simply pressed the triggers, and explosions began to ripple across the previously hidden enemy camp. Two squiggoths collapsed outright as their entrails were blasted across the area, while the orkeosaurus roused itself immediately, bellowing at the new scorch marks along its humongous feet. The warboss roared as well, though partially because it was thrown somewhat by the sudden movement and explosives.

“This is Cyrus-Actual to all squads. Fire upon the enemy, and prioritize your targets properly.”

High above, the thunderhawks began their descent.

10th​ Company Barrage: 13
Thunderhawk Bombing Runs: 7
Cyrus Sniping: 96

It was, certainly, not the best display the 10th​ Company had ever achieved. The explosions going off had kicked up a significant amount of dust, while the orks themselves ran in all directions firing what few guns they had all over the place. Boars squealed and ran back and forth, bowling over their masters and plenty more orks besides, while the squiggoths began to trample all before accidentally slamming into one another. The thunderhawk bombs failed to hit anything significant amongst the enemy, and instead did just the opposite. A tree blown from its bottom collapsed, bringing with it the scouts who had taken it for their own perch, and though none died the snapping of bone and limb were audible to one with ears as finely trained as Cyrus’.

As the warboss roared for order, for a foe to fight, for the simple act of roaring, it revealed itself as the orkeosaurus revealed itself by rearing upwards and pawing in the direction of the thunderhawks. Cyrus narrowed his still organic eye, before sighting and firing in a single snap-shot of his sniper rifle. The open mouth of the warboss, leaning back to scream, atop a rearing orkeosaurus, was perfect enough. The small brain within the ork’s skull disappeared, along with the crown of his head, as the bullet shot directly up through the gullet and into the brainpan. As the ork toppled off of his mount, the death alone seemed to be sensed somehow by the orks as they panicked even further.

Ork Leadership: 55
Surrounded On All Sides: 86

Thankfully, after the initial series of incredibly disappointing shots from his Company, Cyrus felt his spirits lift ever so fractionally as the scouts and neophytes alike re-sighted. More measured blasts started to come from those equipped with bolters, while those who had been granted the usage of sniper rifles started to actually manage to pick off their targets. Squiggoths above were blasted by thunderhawks who actually managed to hit their targets for once, while Cyrus continued his own brutal campaign against every ork possible. A nob with a hammer rose up, thumping its fellows and pointing in the direction of the trees, before the nob’s head was forcibly removed. And so it went, across the entire valley, the orks releasing their pets and creatures in all directions, only to have their precious companions die.

Some of the orks, regardless, managed to reach the treeline, only to find out that simply getting to the vast examples of divergent arboreal life harder to destroy than they might have hoped. Purpled leaves fell as the orks hacked away at the trunks, only for them to be easy targets for the scouts above. Some, Cyrus noted clinically, managed to swing down to the lower branches, firing shotguns at those greenskins who attempted to climb up. It was always good to see space marines that did not forget that it was entirely possible to kill the enemy in more than one way. Too many times had he seen assault marines forget about the possibility of a full bolter, or devastators remiss on their close-combat training. It was why he preferred those of the general populace of a Chapter, the tactical marine, over almost all others.

Almost.

“Cyrus, come in,” Tarkus’ voice crackeld through the vox. “We’ve taken the Black Archipelago, the orks are fleeing in both directions, to Petramis and Pacemen alike.”

“Enemy warboss down, and we are dealing with what appears to be the last core strength of his forces. Hold your position Tarkus.”

“Of course. Fight well, brother.”

“And you.”

Cyrus glared down at the orks, before disappearing into the dust and blood once more. He’d killed the warboss, yes, but needed to capture at least one ork capable of speaking. After all, the Chapter’s records thrived on thoroughness, and without a name…well. It was why he was going to get said name.
